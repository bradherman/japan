## TEAMLAB (Tokyo)

### Which Experience to Choose

There are two teamLab experiences in Tokyo in 2026:

**teamLab Borderless** (Azabudai Hills) -- Reopened Feb 2024 at its new permanent home in Azabudai Hills. Massive, maze-like, projection-based immersive art. You wander freely through rooms where artworks bleed into each other. More visually immersive with projections, lights, LEDs on walls and floors.

**teamLab Planets** (Toyosu) -- Expanded significantly in January 2025 (now similar size to Borderless). More physically immersive: you walk barefoot through ankle-deep water, wade through installations. Follows a more structured, linear path. Open only until end of 2027 (limited-time).

**Recommendation:** Most 2026 reviews say **teamLab Planets is the better pick** -- the water immersion and physical interactivity make it feel more unique. Since Planets closes end of 2027 and Borderless is permanent, Planets gets priority. If you have time and budget for both, do both.

### Ticket Prices

| Venue | Adult Price | Notes |
|-------|-----------|-------|
| teamLab Borderless | ~3,800-4,800 yen (~$25-32 USD) | Dynamic pricing by date/time |
| teamLab Planets | ~3,600-4,800 yen (~$24-32 USD) | Dynamic pricing; weekends/holidays cost more |

Both charge ~200 yen extra if purchased on-site rather than online.

### How to Buy

- **Official website first**: [teamlab.art/e/planets](https://www.teamlab.art/e/planets/) or [teamlab.art/e/tokyo](https://www.teamlab.art/e/tokyo/)
- Timed entry system -- select a specific date and time slot
- Tickets open **~2 months in advance** (so late April slots should be available from late February)
- **Book early** -- Golden Week slots will sell out. Planets entered Guinness World Records in 2024 and same-day tickets are nearly impossible
- Third-party options: Klook, Viator, GetYourGuide (sometimes sell out faster than official site)

### Best Time of Day

- **Weekday + first slot of the day (10:00 AM for Borderless, 8:30 AM for Planets)** = fewest crowds
- Late evening slots (after 7 PM) are also less crowded and have a more atmospheric vibe
- **Avoid**: weekend afternoons and any time during Golden Week peak days (May 2-5)

### How Long Does It Take?

- Borderless: **2-3 hours** (maze-like, easy to spend longer exploring)
- Planets: **2-3 hours** (expanded; the original was 60-90 min but new expansion adds significantly)

### Location & Access

**teamLab Borderless**
- Address: Azabudai Hills Garden Plaza B, B1F, 5-9 Toranomon, Minato-ku, Tokyo
- Nearest station: **Kamiyacho Station** (Tokyo Metro Hibiya Line), Exit 5, 2 min walk
- Also accessible from Azabudai Hills Station (future) and Roppongi-Itchome
- Hours: 10:00-21:00 (EN TEA HOUSE opens 10:30)
- Closed: 1st and 3rd Tuesday of every month

**teamLab Planets**
- Address: 6-1-16 Toyosu, Koto-ku, Tokyo
- Nearest station: **Shin-Toyosu Station** (Yurikamome Line), 1 min walk
- Also accessible from **Toyosu Station** (Tokyo Metro Yurakucho Line), 10 min walk
- Hours: 8:30-22:00
- Open daily (check website for closure dates)

## STUDIO GHIBLI MUSEUM (Mitaka, Tokyo)

### Ticket Availability for Late April 2026

Tickets for **April 2026** go on sale **March 10, 2026 at 10:00 AM JST**. They sell out extremely fast -- often within minutes for popular dates. Golden Week dates (Apr 29-30) will be the hardest to get.

### How to Buy Tickets

1. **Create a Lawson Ticket account NOW** (before March 10) at the international Lawson Ticket website
2. Be logged in and ready at **10:00 AM Japan time on March 10**
3. Select your preferred entry date and time slot
4. Entry times: **10:00, 12:00, 14:00, or 16:00**
5. Pay by credit card; you receive a digital voucher via email
6. Show voucher + passport at entrance on visit day; receive paper ticket

**Important**: No tickets are sold at the museum. Advance reservation is the ONLY way in.

### Ticket Price

- Adults (19+): **1,000 yen** (~$7 USD) -- an absolute bargain
- Ages 13-18: 700 yen
- Ages 7-12: 400 yen
- Ages 4-6: 100 yen

### Location & Getting There

- Address: **1-1-83 Shimorenjaku, Mitaka, Tokyo 181-0013**
- Take the **JR Chuo Line** to **JR Mitaka Station** (~20 min from Shinjuku)
- From Mitaka Station south exit: **15 min walk** through Inokashira Park, or take the community bus (runs every 15 min, ~10 min ride, ~210 yen round trip)
- Hours: **10:00-18:00**
- Closed Tuesdays (check calendar -- may have extra closures)

### Ghibli Museum vs Ghibli Park

| | Ghibli Museum (Mitaka) | Ghibli Park (Nagoya/Aichi) |
|---|---|---|
| Experience | Intimate, art-focused, behind-the-scenes of animation | Walking inside Ghibli film worlds; multiple themed zones |
| Best for | Art lovers, animation fans, adults | Families with kids, those wanting physical immersion |
| Size | Small, cozy | Large, spread across park grounds |
| Access | Easy from Tokyo (30 min from Shinjuku) | 2+ hours from Kyoto/Osaka by train |
| Cost | 1,000 yen | Multiple ticket tiers; more expensive |
| Ticket difficulty | Very hard to get | Also hard but slightly easier |

**Verdict for your trip**: The Ghibli Museum is the better fit -- it's a quick side trip from your Shibuya hotel, it's cheap, and it's the more artistic/adult experience. Ghibli Park is far out of the way (near Nagoya) and more family-oriented. If you're fans but not superfans, the Museum is sufficient and more convenient.

## TSUKIJI OUTER MARKET (Tokyo)

### Best Time to Arrive

- **7:00-7:30 AM** for the most authentic, least crowded experience
- Most stalls open by 7 AM; by 10 AM it's packed
- Most shops close by **noon to 3:00 PM**
- **Closed Wednesdays and Sundays** (some shops vary -- check before going)

### Must-Eat Items & Specific Stalls

| Item | Where | Notes |
|------|-------|-------|
| Fresh sushi/sashimi | **Tsukiji Itadori Bekkan** | Made to order, pricier but worth it |
| Kaisendon (seafood bowl) | Multiple vendors | The signature Tsukiji experience |
| Tamagoyaki (rolled omelet) | **Shouro**, **Marutake**, **Yamachou** | ~4 tamagoyaki shops in the market; a must |
| Grilled giant scallops | **Kakigoya** | Butter, soy sauce, sake; also fresh oysters |
| Grilled eel skewers | **Nisshin Tasuke** | Classic |
| Grilled tuna skewers + beer | **Higashi Indo Maguro Shokai** | Great combo |
| Onigiri (rice balls) | **Marutoyo** | Grilled salmon or cod roe filling |
| Ichigo daifuku (strawberry mochi) | **Soratsuki** | Whole strawberry in bean paste + mochi |
| Uni (sea urchin) | Multiple stalls | Incredibly fresh |

### How Long to Spend

- **2-3 hours** for a solid food crawl
- If you arrive at 7 AM, you can be done by 9:30-10 AM and move on with your day

### Golden Week Tips

- Golden Week will make Tsukiji **significantly more crowded** than usual
- Arrive as early as possible (aim for **7:00 AM or earlier**)
- Many smaller vendors are **cash only** -- bring yen
- Weekday mornings during Golden Week (Apr 30 is a Thursday, May 1 is a Friday) may be slightly less insane than weekend days
- Consider visiting **Apr 25, 26, 27, or 28** (before Golden Week kicks in on Apr 29)

### Location

- Address: 4 Chome-16-2 Tsukiji, Chuo City, Tokyo
- Nearest station: **Tsukiji Station** (Tokyo Metro Hibiya Line) or **Tsukijishijo Station** (Toei Oedo Line)
- ~15 min by train from Shibuya

## SHIBUYA SKY (Tokyo)

### Ticket Prices

| Type | Advance (Online) | At Counter |
|------|------------------|------------|
| Adult | 2,200 yen (~$15) | 2,500 yen |
| Before 3 PM slot | 2,700 yen | -- |
| After 3 PM / sunset slot | 3,400 yen | -- |

### Best Time of Day

- **Sunset is the best time** -- you get daylight views, golden hour, and city lights
- Tokyo sunset in late April / early May: approximately **18:15-18:30**
- Book a slot for **~17:30-18:00** (30-60 min before sunset) to catch the transition
- Evening slots after sunset are also excellent for night views
- Morning is least crowded if you just want clear views without the wait

### How to Buy

- **Advance online booking strongly recommended**, especially during Golden Week
- Official site: [shibuya-sky.tokyo](https://www.shibuya-sky.tokyo/)
- Also available on Klook, KKday, Trip.com
- Timed entry; select your preferred slot
- Golden Week sunset slots will sell out -- book weeks ahead

### How Long to Spend

- **45-90 minutes** is typical
- Indoor galleries + outdoor rooftop observation deck (46th floor, 229m high)
- The outdoor deck is the highlight -- open air, no glass barriers in some areas (great for photos)
- There's a bar on the rooftop (Paradise Lounge) where you can have a drink with the view

### Location

- Inside **Shibuya Scramble Square** building, directly connected to Shibuya Station
- Address: 2-24-12 Shibuya, Shibuya-ku, Tokyo
- Literally above Shibuya Crossing -- you can look straight down at it
- Hours: 10:00-22:30 (last entry 21:20)

## ONSEN NEAR TOKYO

### Thermae-Yu (Shinjuku / Kabukicho)

- Address: 1-1-2 Kabukicho, Shinjuku-ku, Tokyo (right next to Golden Gai)
- Hours: **24 hours** (open all night)
- Admission: **2,700 yen Mon-Thu / 2,800 yen Fri-Sun & holidays** (~$18-19)
- Late night surcharge (midnight-9 AM): additional ~1,836 yen
- Bedrock bath: additional 810 yen

**Tattoo Policy**: Tattoos must be **covered** with seal patches. Seals available for purchase at the facility for 300 yen (max 2 per person). This means it works for **small tattoos only**. If you have large or numerous tattoos, this is NOT a good fit.

**What to Expect**: Multi-floor bath complex with indoor baths, outdoor rotenburo, saunas, relaxation areas. Natural hot spring water. Gender-separated bathing areas. Rental towels, yukata provided. Very popular with tourists due to location and late-night hours.

### Fully Tattoo-Friendly Options (No Cover Required)

**Konparu-yu** (Ginza)
- Historic sento operating since 1863 in the Edo-period style
- **Fully tattoo-friendly** -- no covering required, all sizes welcome
- Address: 8-7-5 Ginza, Chuo-ku, Tokyo
- Affordable traditional sento pricing (~500-520 yen)
- A rare, authentic experience in the heart of Ginza

**Daikoku-yu** (Oshiage / near Tokyo Skytree)
- **Fully tattoo-friendly** -- all sizes, no covering required. Just inform staff on arrival as a courtesy
- Located in Sumida ward near Skytree
- Traditional bathhouse with modern amenities
- Affordable sento pricing

**Jakotsu-yu** (Asakusa)
- Natural black mineral water from the Paleozoic era
- Tattoo-friendly
- Located in Asakusa, easy to combine with temple sightseeing
- Known for water that "warms you to the core"

### Private Onsen Option

If large tattoos are a concern, consider booking a **private onsen (kashikiri)** at a ryokan or spa. Many places rent private baths by the hour for couples. Your Hakone ryokan (**Hakone Gora Byakudan**) likely has private in-room onsen -- worth confirming.

## NARA DAY TRIP (from Kyoto)

Based on your itinerary, you're in Kyoto Apr 30 - May 4, with a Nara day trip planned for May 1, 2, or 3.

### Getting There

| Route | Time | Cost | Notes |
|-------|------|------|-------|
| **Kintetsu Limited Express** (Kyoto Station to Kintetsu-Nara) | **~35 min** | ~1,490 yen | **Best option** -- Kintetsu-Nara Station is closer to the park |
| JR Nara Line (Kyoto Station to JR Nara) | ~45-50 min | ~720 yen (covered by JR Pass) | Cheaper but station is further from deer park |

**Recommendation**: Take the Kintetsu Limited Express. Kintetsu-Nara Station drops you much closer to the deer park and main sights. The extra cost is worth the convenience.

### Must-See Spots

1. **Nara Park (Deer Park)** -- Hundreds of free-roaming deer. Buy shika senbei (deer crackers) for ~200 yen/pack (buy 2 packs -- they go fast). The deer bow if you bow to them. Watch out -- they can be aggressive about food!

2. **Todai-ji Temple** -- Houses the world's largest bronze Buddha (15m tall) inside the world's largest wooden building. Absolutely jaw-dropping. Entry: ~600 yen

3. **Kasuga Taisha Shrine** -- Famous for thousands of stone and bronze lanterns. Beautiful forested path. Entry to inner shrine: ~500 yen

4. **Naramachi** -- Historic merchant district with traditional machiya houses, cafes, and shops. Good for lunch and wandering

5. **Kofuku-ji Temple** -- Five-story pagoda near the station, free to view from outside

### How Long to Spend

- **Half day (4-5 hours)** covers the highlights: deer park, Todai-ji, Kasuga Taisha
- **Full day (6-7 hours)** adds Naramachi, Kofuku-ji, leisurely lunch, more exploring
- For your trip: a half day is plenty if you want a relaxed pace. Leave by 8-9 AM, back in Kyoto by early-mid afternoon

### Golden Week Timing

- **May 1 (Friday) is probably your best bet** -- May 2-5 are the peak Golden Week days
- Arrive **before 10 AM** -- crowds pour in after that
- Even during Golden Week, Nara is less insanely crowded than Kyoto's main temples
- The deeper paths at Kasuga Taisha and further parts of the park remain relatively peaceful

## HAKONE (May 7-9, staying at Hakone Gora Byakudan)

You have 1.5 days here. Brad has foot surgery recovery, so no hiking. The ryokan itself should be the main event (onsen, relaxation), with easy outings.

### Hakone Open-Air Museum

- Address: 1121 Ninotaira, Hakone-machi, Ashigarashimo-gun, Kanagawa
- Hours: **9:00-17:00** (last entry 16:30), open year-round
- Admission: **1,500 yen** adults (100 yen discount with Hakone Free Pass)
- Japan's first open-air sculpture museum; 70,000+ sq ft of gardens
- Major Picasso collection
- **Natural hot spring foot bath** on the grounds (free with admission; towels 100 yen) -- perfect for resting feet
- Flat, paved paths throughout -- no hiking required
- Allow **2-3 hours**

### Lake Ashi Pirate Ship Cruise

- Three pirate-themed sightseeing ships cruise Lake Ashi
- Route: Togendai - Hakone-Machi - Moto-Hakone
- One-way: ~1,200-1,500 yen / Round-trip: ~2,000-2,500 yen
- Special cabin upgrade: +500-1,000 yen
- On clear days: views of Mt. Fuji reflected in the lake
- The Queen Ashinoko features Hakone's traditional yosegi-zaiku woodcraft patterns
- **Included in Hakone Free Pass**
- Allow ~30-40 min one way

### Hakone Ropeway

- Connects Togendai (lake) to Owakudani (volcanic valley) to Sounzan
- ~20 min ride; round-trip: ~2,550 yen
- Views of volcanic steam vents and (on clear days) Mt. Fuji
- At Owakudani: eat the famous **black eggs** (kuro-tamago) -- eggs boiled in sulfuric hot springs, said to add 7 years to your life. ~500 yen for a bag of 5
- **Included in Hakone Free Pass**
- Note: Ropeway occasionally closes due to volcanic activity -- check status before going

### Hakone Free Pass

- **Strongly recommended**: 2-day pass from Odawara costs ~5,000 yen (adult)
- Covers: pirate ship, ropeway, Hakone Tozan Railway, buses, and more
- Discounts at Open-Air Museum and other attractions
- Since you're arriving via Shinkansen to Odawara, this is the ideal pass

### Suggested 1.5-Day Plan (No Hiking)

**Day 1 (May 7, afternoon arrival)**
- Arrive Odawara ~12:55 PM, take Hakone Tozan Railway/bus to ryokan
- Check in, enjoy the ryokan's onsen and facilities
- Evening: kaiseki dinner at ryokan (usually included)

**Day 2 (May 8, full day)**
- Morning: Hakone Open-Air Museum (2-3 hours, including foot bath)
- Afternoon: Take ropeway to Owakudani, eat black eggs, enjoy views
- Continue to Togendai, take pirate ship cruise on Lake Ashi
- Return to ryokan for onsen and dinner
- All of this is easy, flat, no hiking -- just transportation between scenic stops

**Day 3 (May 9, morning departure)**
- Morning onsen soak
- Depart for Tokyo

## OSAKA (May 4-7, staying at Cross Hotel Osaka)

### Best Neighborhoods to Explore

**Dotonbori** (your hotel is right here!)
- Cross Hotel Osaka is in the heart of Dotonbori/Shinsaibashi
- Iconic neon-lit canal, Glico Running Man sign, mechanical Kani Doraku crab
- Best food street in Japan -- takoyaki, okonomiyaki, kushikatsu everywhere
- Peak energy: **5-11 PM** when neon signs light up
- **Hozenji Yokocho**: atmospheric stone-paved alley with lanterns, right behind the noise. Visit the moss-covered Mizukake Fudo statue. Less touristy, more romantic
- Walk along the Dotonbori canal at night

**Shinsekai**
- Retro working-class neighborhood built in 1912 as "Osaka's New York"
- **Tsutenkaku Tower**: 103m observation deck, 900 yen adults (10 AM - 8 PM)
- The kushikatsu (deep-fried skewers) capital -- don't double-dip!
- Retro arcades, dive bars, personality-packed atmosphere
- Best time: **5-11 PM** when neon signs blaze
- ~15 min by train from Dotonbori

**Shinsaibashi**
- Connected to Dotonbori; long covered shopping arcade
- Fashion, boutiques, department stores
- Good for shopping by day, then walk south into Dotonbori for dinner

**Kuromon Market** ("Osaka's Kitchen")
- Fresh seafood, street food, and produce
- Similar concept to Tsukiji but Osaka-style
- Best visited in the morning
- ~10 min walk from your hotel

**Nakanoshima**
- Island between two rivers; Osaka's cultural center
- Nakanoshima Museum of Art (modern art -- right up your alley)
- Beautiful riverside walks, especially at night

### Must-See Attractions

1. **Osaka Castle** -- Iconic landmark, beautiful grounds and moat. Interior is a museum (elevator access, no hiking). Free to walk the grounds; castle tower entry ~600 yen. Allow 1.5-2 hours
2. **Dotonbori Canal Walk** -- The quintessential Osaka experience, especially at night
3. **Shinsekai + Tsutenkaku Tower** -- Retro Osaka vibes
4. **Kuromon Market** -- Morning food crawl
5. **Nakanoshima Museum of Art** -- If you want a modern art fix

### 40th Birthday (May 6) Ideas

Your birthday dinner is already booked: **Matsuzaka Gyu Yakiniku M Hozenji-Hanare at 7 PM** -- great choice, right in Hozenji Yokocho.

Additional birthday ideas for the day:

- **Yakatabune dinner cruise on the Okawa River**: Traditional Japanese boat with kaiseki dinner, sake, and night views of illuminated Osaka. Bookable through Klook/KKday. Could be a pre-birthday evening activity on May 5 instead, since dinner is booked May 6
- **Umeda Sky Building floating garden observatory**: 40th-floor open-air observation deck with 360-degree views. Especially stunning at sunset/night. ~1,500 yen. Great birthday photo spot
- **Cocktail bars in Kitashinchi**: Osaka's upscale bar district, just north of Nakanoshima. World-class cocktail bars in tiny spaces. Perfect for a birthday nightcap after yakiniku
- **Hozenji Yokocho after dinner**: Since your restaurant is right there, walk the lantern-lit alley, splash water on the moss Buddha for good luck, find a tiny bar

## KYOTO (Apr 30 - May 4, staying at Hotel Forza Kyoto Shijo Kawaramachi)

Your hotel is in a great central location -- Shijo Kawaramachi is the heart of Kyoto's downtown, walking distance to Gion and Higashiyama.

### Fushimi Inari -- Best Time to Avoid Crowds

- The shrine is **open 24 hours** -- it never closes
- **Best time: Before 7:00 AM or after 8:00 PM**
- By 8 AM crowds start arriving; by 10 AM it's packed (worse during Golden Week)
- Trains start at ~5:30 AM; aim to arrive **6:00-7:00 AM** for near-empty torii gates
- Evening visits after dark have an atmospheric, slightly eerie quality with lit lanterns
- The full trail to the summit involves significant uphill walking (~2-3 hours round trip). **For Brad's foot**: the first section of torii gates (Senbon Torii) is the iconic photo spot and only takes 15-20 min of walking. You don't need to summit
- Station: **Inari Station** (JR Nara Line), the shrine entrance is literally outside the station exit
- Free admission

### Must-See Temples & Shrines

**Kiyomizu-dera**
- Iconic wooden stage jutting out over a hillside with sweeping city views
- Founded 778, UNESCO World Heritage Site
- Entry: ~400 yen
- Hours: 6:00 AM - 6:00 PM (varies seasonally)
- **Go early** (opening time) during Golden Week
- Note: involves uphill walk through Higashiyama streets (not steep, but steady)
- Address: 1-294 Kiyomizu, Higashiyama-ku

**Kinkaku-ji (Golden Pavilion)**
- Gold-leaf-covered pavilion reflected in a mirror pond
- One of Kyoto's most iconic sights
- Entry: ~500 yen
- Hours: 9:00 AM - 5:00 PM
- Best in morning for reflections and light
- Address: 1 Kinkakuji-cho, Kita-ku (~30 min by bus from your hotel)

**Yasaka Shrine**
- Gateway between downtown and Higashiyama
- Free, open 24 hours
- Beautiful stone torii gate; lantern-lit at night
- Right at the east end of Shijo-dori -- walkable from your hotel

**Kodai-ji Temple**
- Beautiful Zen temple with stunning gardens
- ~15 min walk from Gion
- Entry: ~600 yen
- Known for weeping cherry blossoms (might still have late blooms)

**Ryoan-ji**
- Famous Zen rock garden (15 stones, raked gravel)
- Entry: ~500 yen
- Peaceful, contemplative experience
- Address: 13 Ryoanji Goryonoshitacho, Ukyo-ku

**Nanzen-ji**
- Beautiful brick aqueduct (great photo spot), free to see
- Temple grounds: free; inner buildings: ~600 yen
- Less crowded than Kiyomizu-dera

### Best Neighborhoods to Wander

**Gion** (walkable from your hotel)
- Kyoto's famous geisha district
- Traditional wooden machiya teahouses
- Best at dusk -- slim chance of spotting a maiko (apprentice geisha) heading to an appointment
- **Hanamikoji-dori**: main street lined with teahouses
- Note: respect the "no photography of geisha" signs

**Higashiyama**
- Stone-paved lanes of **Ninenzaka** and **Sannenzaka** (Instagrammable but genuinely beautiful)
- Connect Yasaka Shrine area to Kiyomizu-dera
- Traditional shops, tea houses, matcha dessert spots
- Best combined with Kiyomizu-dera visit in the early morning

**Pontocho**
- Narrow alley running parallel to the Kamogawa River
- Lined with restaurants and bars; many have riverside terraces (kawayuka -- seasonal dining platforms over the river, typically open May-September)
- **Walkable from your hotel** -- perfect for evening strolling and dinner
- In May, the kawayuka terraces should be open!

**Nishiki Market** ("Kyoto's Kitchen")
- Covered market street, ~5 min walk from your hotel
- ~130 shops selling pickles, tofu, sweets, knives, ceramics
- Good for grazing and souvenir shopping
- Go in the morning for fewer crowds

**Philosopher's Path**
- Canal-side walking path connecting Nanzen-ji to Ginkaku-ji
- Peaceful, tree-lined, ~2 km (flat, easy walk -- fine for Brad)
- Lovely even after cherry blossom season

### Arashiyama Bamboo Grove -- Worth It During Golden Week?

**Honest assessment**: The bamboo grove itself is small and gets extremely crowded, especially during Golden Week. It's a quick walk-through (10-15 min), not a destination on its own.

**Verdict**: Skip Arashiyama during Golden Week unless you are willing to go **before 8:00 AM**. The crowds between 10 AM - 3 PM during Golden Week will be oppressive. If you do go:
- Arrive by **7:00 AM** for near-empty paths and good photos
- Combine with: Tenryu-ji Temple (beautiful garden), Togetsukyo Bridge, monkey park (but monkey park involves uphill climb -- skip for Brad's foot)
- The train ride out on the Sagano Scenic Railway is nice

**Alternative**: Spend that time in Gion/Higashiyama or Fushimi Inari at dawn -- more rewarding with fewer crowd headaches.
