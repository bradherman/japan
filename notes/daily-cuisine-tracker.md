## Strategy Notes

### Cuisine Distribution Goals
- **Ramen**: 3-4 times (different styles: tsukemen, tantanmen, shoyu, wagyu)
- **Sushi**: 3-4 times (mix of budget, mid-range, high-end)
- **Kaiseki**: 3 times (2x ryokan + 1-2x restaurant)
- **Unique to location**: Takoyaki/okonomiyaki in Osaka, kaiseki in Kyoto
- **Special occasions**: French/teppanyaki/yakiniku for celebrations
- **Street food**: Tsukiji, Nishiki Market, Yanaka Ginza on travel days

### Variety Checks
- No back-to-back ramen/noodles
- No consecutive fried foods (tonkatsu → kushikatsu → tempura)
- Balance heavy meals (yakiniku, teppanyaki) with lighter options
- Strategic cafe/bakery breakfasts to maximize lunch/dinner variety
- Pizza and burgers as palate cleansers from Japanese cuisine

### Reservation Priorities
1. ✅ **May 6 Yakiniku M** - Already booked
2. **Tonkatsu Narikura** - Reservation required
3. **Bird Land Ginza** - Michelin, popular
4. **Sushi SHIN / Udatsu** - High-end sushi reservations
5. **The Pizza Bar on 38th** - World-ranked pizza
6. **Quintessence** - 3-star French (if splurging)

### Weekday-Only Opportunities
- **Udon Maruka**: Mon-Fri only (best in Tokyo!)
- **Nakajima Curry**: Weekday lunch special
