# Packing List — Alyona

**Trip:** 15 nights (Apr 25 - May 10, 2026) | Tokyo → Kyoto → Osaka → Hakone → Tokyo
**Bag strategy:** 1 carry-on roller + 1 mid-size daypack. 1-2 collapsible duffels packed flat for purchases to check on the way home.
**Weather:** 10-25°C (50-77°F). Cool mornings/evenings, warm afternoons. Rain likely 2-4 days. Humid.
**Laundry plan:** Do laundry every 4-5 days. All hotels have coin laundry or are near a coin laundromat.

---

## Clothes — Tops (6-7)

- [ ] 1 nice blouse or dressy top (for teppanyaki/omakase/birthday dinner — something that packs small and doesn't wrinkle)
- [ ] 3-4 lightweight tops (mix of tees and tanks — moisture-wicking preferred for 15-25K step days)
- [ ] 1 long-sleeve lightweight layer (cardigan, merino pullover, or linen shirt — doubles as evening layer and temple cover-up)
- [ ] 1 light jacket (packable rain jacket with hood — handles rain + cool evenings; Patagonia Houdini or similar)

## Clothes — Bottoms (3-4)

- [ ] 2 pairs lightweight pants (one darker pair works for nice dinners — linen, ponte, or travel-weight)
- [ ] 1 pair leggings or joggers (travel days, ryokan lounging, casual walking)
- [ ] 1 skirt or dress (optional — versatile for warm days and dinner; easy to dress up or down)

## Clothes — Underwear & Socks (5-6 each)

- [ ] 5-6 underwear (quick-dry preferred — can hand-wash and air dry overnight)
- [ ] 2-3 bras (1 everyday + 1-2 sport/bralette for walking days)
- [ ] 5-6 pairs socks (mix of: no-show for warm days, light crew for walking days)
- [ ] 1 pair compression socks (for the 10+ hr flights)

## Shoes (2-3)

- [ ] 1 pair comfortable walking shoes — SLIP-ON or easy on/off strongly recommended (you remove shoes at temples, ryokans, some restaurants, fitting rooms). Allbirds, On Cloud, or similar. Break them in before the trip.
- [ ] 1 pair sandals or slides (ryokan, hotel room, Hakone onsen area — doubles as shower shoes)
- [ ] 1 pair dressier flats (optional — if you want a nicer shoe for dinners, something comfortable to walk in)

## Outerwear / Layers

- [ ] Light packable rain jacket (see above — do NOT skip this, April/May rain is real)
- [ ] Light scarf or wrap (useful for temples, AC-blasted restaurants, or dressing up a casual outfit)
- [ ] Small compact umbrella (or buy a clear vinyl one at any 7-Eleven for ¥500)

---

## Daypack (Daily Carry)

- [ ] Phone charger + cable
- [ ] Portable battery pack (10,000+ mAh — long days, lots of Google Maps)
- [ ] Small quick-dry towel or handkerchief — **Japan-specific: many public restrooms don't have paper towels or hand dryers.** Carry a small towel daily.
- [ ] Reusable water bottle (tap water is safe and excellent in Japan)
- [ ] Sunglasses
- [ ] Small zip pouch for daily cash + coins (you'll accumulate 100-yen coins fast)
- [ ] Small crossbody bag or belt bag (for easy access to IC card, phone, cash while walking)

---

## Toiletries

*Strategy: Pack minimal, buy the rest at a Japanese drugstore (Matsumoto Kiyoshi, Sundrug, or any conbini). Japan has incredible toiletry and skincare options — half the fun is browsing.*

### Bring from Home
- [ ] Matador silicone bottles filled with: shampoo, conditioner, body wash
- [ ] Skincare basics in travel sizes (cleanser, moisturizer, serum)
- [ ] Travel-size toothpaste
- [ ] Toothbrush
- [ ] Deodorant (bring your own — Japanese deodorant is very mild/different)
- [ ] Sunscreen (SPF 50 — or buy Japanese sunscreen: Biore UV Aqua Rich is cult favorite, ¥600-800 at any drugstore)
- [ ] Lip balm with SPF
- [ ] Minimal makeup (tinted moisturizer/BB cream, mascara, brow pencil — keep it light for humid weather)
- [ ] Hair ties and clips
- [ ] Dry shampoo (travel size — for days between washes)

### Buy in Japan (or Bring if You Prefer)
- [ ] Sheet masks (Japanese ones are world-class and cheap — stock up at any drugstore)
- [ ] Face mist/setting spray
- [ ] Blotting papers (humid weather essential — ¥100-300 everywhere)
- [ ] Cotton pads
- [ ] Micellar water or cleansing oil

---

## Medicine & Health

- [ ] Tylenol / acetaminophen (headaches, general pain)
- [ ] Ibuprofen (cramps, inflammation)
- [ ] Gas-X / simethicone (new foods, rich meals)
- [ ] Imodium / loperamide (just in case)
- [ ] Antihistamine (Zyrtec/Claritin — spring pollen is aggressive in Japan)
- [ ] Band-aids / blister pads (Compeed or similar — critical for 20K+ step days)
- [ ] Any prescription medications (bring more than you need + keep in original bottles for customs)
- [ ] Melatonin (jet lag recovery — Japan is 16 hours ahead of PDT)

---

## Tech & Electronics

- [ ] Phone
- [ ] Phone charger + cable
- [ ] Portable battery pack + charging cable
- [ ] AirPods / headphones
- [ ] Camera (optional — phone is probably fine)
- [ ] **Japan uses Type A plugs (same as US) at 100V.** No adapter needed. All modern chargers handle 100-240V automatically.

---

## Travel Documents & Essentials

- [ ] Passport (valid 6+ months past May 10)
- [ ] Physical credit/debit cards (Visa/Mastercard work best — tell your bank about Japan travel)
- [ ] Cash: Withdraw ¥30,000-50,000 at Narita airport ATM on arrival. Many small restaurants, bars, and market stalls are cash-only.
- [ ] Printed hotel confirmations (optional but nice for taxi drivers — show the hotel name in Japanese)
- [ ] Travel insurance info
- [ ] Copy of passport (photo on phone + paper copy in bag)

---

## Packing Accessories

- [ ] 1-2 collapsible duffels (packed flat — for checking purchased goods on return flight)
- [ ] 1-2 packing cubes (keeps carry-on organized)
- [ ] 1 small laundry bag (for dirty clothes between washes)
- [ ] 1 zip-lock bag for liquids (TSA 3-1-1 on outbound flight)
- [ ] Luggage lock (TSA-approved)

---

## Nice-to-Have (Not Essential)

- [ ] Packable tote bag or eco bag (Japan charges for plastic bags; great for konbini runs and market shopping)
- [ ] Notebook + pen
- [ ] Sleep mask + earplugs (flights + thin-walled hotels)
- [ ] Kindle or book (flights and bullet trains)
- [ ] Small jewelry pouch (for a few pairs of earrings or a necklace)

---

## DO NOT PACK

- **Bulky jeans** — heavy, slow to dry, take up too much bag space. Lightweight pants do double duty.
- **High heels** — you'll be walking 15-25K steps a day on uneven surfaces. Cute flats only.
- **Full-size toiletries** — buy there or use travel bottles. Japanese drugstores are incredible.
- **Too many shoes** — 2-3 pairs max. Every extra shoe eats carry-on space you need for purchases.
- **Anything precious** — leave expensive jewelry at home. Keep it simple.

---

## Pre-Departure Checklist

- [ ] Notify bank/credit card of Japan travel dates
- [ ] Download offline Google Maps for Tokyo, Kyoto, Osaka, Hakone
- [ ] Set up Apple Wallet Suica (or buy physical card at Narita)
- [ ] Download Google Translate (with Japanese offline pack)
- [ ] Confirm travel insurance covers Japan
- [ ] Charge all devices + battery pack night before
- [ ] Weigh carry-on bag — leave room for purchases
