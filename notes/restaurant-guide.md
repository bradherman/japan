# Japan Restaurant Guide

Comprehensive restaurant research for Tokyo (Apr 25-30, May 9-10), Kyoto (Apr 30-May 4), and Osaka (May 4-7).

**Golden Week Warning (Apr 29 - May 6):** Your trip falls squarely within Golden Week, Japan's busiest holiday period. Many small family-run restaurants close for several days during this period so their staff can take vacation. Chain restaurants and hotel restaurants generally stay open. Crowds will be significant at popular spots. Book reservations as far in advance as possible -- 3-6 months out is recommended for popular spots. Travel peaks on May 2 (people leaving cities) and May 5-6 (returning). Confirm hours with any restaurant before visiting during this period.

## TOKYO (Apr 25-30, May 9-10)

### 1. RAMEN

#### Ginza Hachigo (Chukasoba Ginza Hachigou)
- **Address:** 3-14-2 Ginza, Chuo-ku, Tokyo (274m from Higashi-Ginza Station, exit A2)
- **Hours:** Wed-Sun from 11:00am; selected Tuesdays. Walk-ins 11:00-12:30; reservations 12:30-14:30
- **Closed:** Mon, Tue (most weeks)
- **Price:** ~1,200-1,400 yen/pp
- **Reservations:** Online via TableCheck. Slots open every Saturday at 9am for the following week, half-hour intervals from 12:30-14:30. 500 yen booking fee/pp. Walk-ins accepted 11:00-12:30 but require arriving early and waiting in line.
- **Payment:** Cards accepted
- **What to order:** The "Ravioli Gourmandise Chuka Soba" -- features ravioli stuffed with truffle and foie gras atop clear, golden broth made with chicken, clams, dried tomatoes, shiitake, kelp, and prosciutto. Unlike any ramen you have had.
- **Notes:** Only 6 counter seats. Was Michelin-starred (Bib Gourmand in 2026 guide). This is a refined, French-influenced ramen experience. The chef previously worked in French cuisine. Not your typical ramen -- this is a destination bowl.
- **Golden Week:** Likely open (check TableCheck), but slots will book instantly on Saturday mornings.

#### Nakiryu (Sosakumenkobo Nakiryu)
- **Address:** 2-34-4 Minamiotsuka, Toshima-ku, Tokyo (SKY Minamiotsuka 1F, near Otsuka Station south exit)
- **Hours:** Lunch 11:30-15:00, Dinner 18:00-21:00
- **Closed:** Tuesdays
- **Price:** ~1,000-1,500 yen/pp
- **Reservations:** None accepted. Walk-in only. Arrive by 10:30am for lunch to minimize wait.
- **Payment:** Cash only
- **What to order:** Tantanmen (dan dan noodle) -- their signature. Rich sesame-chili broth with perfectly balanced heat and nuttiness. The shio ramen is also excellent for something cleaner.
- **Notes:** Held a Michelin star 2017-2023, now Bib Gourmand. Only 10 seats. Wait times range from 30 min to 2.5 hours. Go on a weekday and arrive early. This is the best spicy ramen in Tokyo.
- **Golden Week:** Will be extremely crowded. Consider going May 9 (after GW) instead if you return to Tokyo.

#### Konjiki Hototogisu (Sobahouse Konjiki Hototogisu)
- **Address:** 2-4-1 Shinjuku, Shinjuku-ku, Tokyo (Miyaniwa Mansion 1F, room 105, near Shinjuku-Gyoenmae Station)
- **Hours:** Lunch 11:30-15:00, Dinner 18:30-22:00
- **Closed:** Check current schedule (varies)
- **Price:** ~1,100-1,500 yen/pp
- **Reservations:** None. Walk-in only with queue.
- **Payment:** Cash only (vending machine ticket system)
- **What to order:** Shio soba -- the signature bowl. Broth is an artisanal blend of 60+ ingredients including clam, truffle oil, and porcini. Also try the shoyu soba.
- **Notes:** Previously Michelin-starred, now Bib Gourmand. Only 7 counter seats + 2 small tables. Convenient location near Shinjuku Gyoen park. Sophisticated, umami-bomb ramen -- truffle oil adds elegance.
- **Golden Week:** Expect longer queues than usual.

#### Fuunji
- **Address:** 2-14-3 Yoyogi, Shibuya-ku, Tokyo (5 min walk from Shinjuku Station south exit)
- **Hours:** 11:00-15:00, 17:00-21:00
- **Closed:** Sundays
- **Price:** ~1,200-1,450 yen/pp
- **Reservations:** None. Walk-in only with queue.
- **Payment:** Cash (vending machine)
- **What to order:** Special Tsukemen (dipping noodles). Rich, intense fish-based broth for dipping thick noodles. The broth is viscous and deeply savory -- possibly the best tsukemen in Tokyo.
- **Notes:** Very convenient to your Shibuya hotel. Perpetual queue but it moves fast. Outstanding for tsukemen specifically. Get the special size (400g) if hungry.
- **Golden Week:** Closed Sundays; weekday visits recommended.

#### Mugi to Olive (Bonus pick - if in Ginza area)
- **Address:** 6-12-12 Ginza, Chuo-ku, Tokyo
- **Hours:** 11:00-15:30, 17:30-22:00 (varies)
- **Closed:** Sundays
- **Price:** ~1,000-1,400 yen/pp
- **What to order:** Clam Shio Soba -- elegant, light broth fragrant with clam and olive oil. Feels more like a fine consomme than typical ramen.
- **Notes:** Upscale feel, Western-style exterior. Great pairing with Ginza Hachigo if you want a ramen crawl in Ginza.

### 2. AFFORDABLE SUSHI (50-80/pp range, ~7,000-12,000 yen)

#### Sushi Yoshikawa (Ebisu)
- **Address:** 1-6-3 Ebisu, Shibuya City, Tokyo (5 min from Ebisu Station)
- **Hours:** Lunch and dinner (reservation required)
- **Price:** ~11,000 yen/pp (~$80)
- **Reservations:** Required. Book via phone or Tabelog.
- **What to order:** 17-piece omakase course. The signature "dragon ball roll" with otoro and uni is the showstopper — one of the most photogenic pieces of sushi in Tokyo. Finished with an unagi hand roll.
- **Notes:** Viral on Instagram for the dragon ball roll. At ¥11,000 it's excellent value for the quality and variety. Ebisu is convenient from your Shibuya hotel. A strong contender for your one special sushi meal.

#### Sushi Matsumoto (Shibuya)
- **Address:** Near Shibuya Station (confirm exact address via Tabelog)
- **Hours:** Lunch and dinner
- **Price:** Lunch Nigiri Set from 5,000 yen (~$33/pp)
- **Reservations:** Recommended
- **What to order:** Lunch Nigiri Set -- 10+ pieces of seasonal nigiri. Excellent quality for the price.
- **Notes:** Peaceful atmosphere despite proximity to Shibuya Crossing. Great value lunch option near your hotel.

#### Midori Sushi (Akasaka or Shibuya Seibu branch)
- **Address:** Multiple locations; Shibuya location on 8F of Shibuya Seibu department store is most convenient for you
- **Hours:** Lunch and dinner; closed Jan 1 only
- **Price:** Lunch ~2,000-3,000 yen/pp, Dinner ~5,000-6,000 yen/pp
- **Reservations:** No reservations. Queue only. Arrive before 11am opening.
- **Payment:** Cards accepted
- **What to order:** Omakase nigiri set, or order a la carte for the best seasonal fish. The toro is famously generous.
- **Notes:** Legendary for quality-to-price ratio. Experienced sushi chefs serving serious sushi at casual prices. Long waits at peak times (30-90 min). The Shibuya branch queue overlooks the crossing. Multiple Tokyo locations.
- **Golden Week:** Expect 60-90 min waits.

#### Iki na Sushi Dokoro Abe (Roppongi)
- **Address:** Roppongi, Tokyo (15 min walk from Roppongi Station)
- **Hours:** Lunch and dinner
- **Closed:** Never
- **Price:** Lunch from 3,000 yen/pp, Dinner from 10,000 yen/pp
- **Reservations:** Available
- **Payment:** Cards accepted
- **What to order:** Lunch course or a la carte -- authentic Edomae style at reasonable prices.
- **Notes:** From the Ritz-Carlton concierge list. Authentic ambience, experienced itamae (chef).

#### Tsukiji Sushi Omakase (Tsukiji area)
- **Address:** 7 min walk from Tsukiji Station
- **Hours:** Check current schedule
- **Price:** 22-piece omakase from ~8,000-10,000 yen/pp
- **Reservations:** Required
- **What to order:** Their 22-piece omakase -- unusually generous for the price. Menu changes monthly.

### 3. HIGH-END SUSHI (~100-125/pp, ~15,000-18,000 yen)

#### Sushi SHIN (1 Michelin Star) -- BEST VALUE PICK
- **Address:** Nishiazabu, Minato-ku, Tokyo
- **Hours:** Lunch (Thu-Sun), Dinner nightly
- **Closed:** Mondays; no lunch Tue & Wed
- **Price:** Lunch omakase from ~18,150 yen/pp (~$120). This is your target price point.
- **Reservations:** Required. Book via phone (03-5485-0031), WhatsApp (+81-80-6219-9295), email (reservation-tokyo@sushishin.jp), or through the website at sushishin-tokyo.jp/reservations
- **Payment:** Cards accepted
- **What to order:** Lunch omakase course. Chef Shintaro Suzuki's self-taught Edomae style with personal inspiration. 10+ consecutive years of Michelin stars.
- **Notes:** Best value Michelin sushi at lunch in your price range. International clientele means English-friendly. Book well in advance. 72-hour cancellation policy.
- **Golden Week:** Book NOW for late April dates. Will sell out.

#### Udatsu Sushi (1 Michelin Star) -- RUNNER-UP
- **Address:** 2-48-10 Kamimeguro, Meguro-ku, Tokyo (5 min walk from Nakameguro Station)
- **Hours:** Mon-Fri lunch at 12:00 (single seating); Sat/Sun/holidays at 11:00 and 13:00; Dinner at 18:00 and 21:00
- **Closed:** Never, but chef is absent Wed/Sun/holidays (sous-chef serves)
- **Price:** Lunch from 14,400 yen/pp (~$95); Dinner from 24,000 yen/pp
- **Reservations:** Required. Book via OpenTable or email.
- **Payment:** Cards accepted
- **What to order:** Omakase lunch -- "Art x Sushi" concept with concrete gallery walls and art pieces. Seasonal ingredients change monthly.
- **Notes:** At ~$95 for lunch, this is actually under your budget. Beautiful space, Nakameguro neighborhood is lovely for a walk. Book on a day the head chef is present (Mon/Tue/Thu/Fri/Sat).
- **Golden Week:** Book immediately for any remaining availability.

### 4. UDON

#### Udon Maruka
- **Address:** 3-16-1 Kanda Ogawamachi, Chiyoda-ku, Tokyo (New Surugadai Building 1F, Jimbocho area)
- **Hours:** Mon-Fri 11:00-16:00, 17:30-19:30; Sat 11:00-14:30
- **Closed:** Sundays and national holidays
- **Price:** ~500-800 yen/pp
- **Reservations:** None. Walk-in only. People line up an hour before opening.
- **Payment:** CASH ONLY
- **What to order:** Kamaage udon (served in hot water with dipping sauce) or Bukkake udon. The noodles are Sanuki-style -- thick, impossibly chewy, with a satisfying bite. Considered the gold standard for udon in Tokyo.
- **Notes:** Closes when noodles run out (often well before listed closing time). Arrive early. Arguably the single best udon experience in all of Tokyo.
- **Golden Week:** CLOSED on national holidays (Apr 29, May 3-6). Plan for a weekday visit Apr 25-28 or May 9.

#### Udon Shin
- **Address:** Near Shin-Ochanomizu Station, Tokyo (confirm via Google Maps)
- **Hours:** Lunch and dinner (check current hours)
- **Price:** ~800-1,500 yen/pp
- **Reservations:** None. Digital queue number system; expect 30+ min wait.
- **Payment:** Cash preferred
- **What to order:** Curry udon or the tempura udon combo. Handcrafted noodles with perfect chew. The crispy tempura complements the rich broth beautifully.
- **Notes:** Another top-tier Tokyo udon spot. The digital queue system lets you explore nearby while waiting.

#### Udon Oniyanma (Gotanda)
- **Address:** Gotanda Station area, Tokyo
- **Hours:** Varies by location
- **Price:** ~400-800 yen/pp
- **Reservations:** None (standing/quick-eat format)
- **Payment:** Cash
- **What to order:** Cold udon with seasonal tempura. Simple, perfect execution. The chikuwa (fish cake) tempura is legendary.
- **Notes:** Standing-style tachigui (stand-and-eat). Ultra-fast, ultra-cheap, ultra-good. Multiple locations.

### 6. YAKITORI & SKEWERS

#### Omoide Yokocho (Memory Lane) -- Your hotel is right here
- **Address:** West side of Shinjuku Station (1 min walk from west exit)
- **Hours:** Most stalls open ~17:00-midnight; some open for lunch
- **Closed:** Individual stalls vary; most open daily
- **Price:** 1,000-2,500 yen/pp for skewers + drinks
- **Reservations:** None. Squeeze in where you can.
- **Payment:** CASH ONLY at most stalls
- **What to order:** Everything grilled -- chicken hearts, gizzards, skin, thigh (momo), and negima (chicken and leek). Try motsu (offal) if you are adventurous. Pair with beer or highball.
- **Specific stall tip:** Yasubee is one of the most iconic -- the owner is used to foreigners and the yakitori is solid. For a more local vibe, wander deeper into the alleys away from the main drag.
- **Notes:** Dave specifically flagged this as "right by the hotel." Perfect for a casual first-night dinner after arriving from Narita. 70+ tiny stalls. Elbow-to-elbow communal seating. The atmosphere IS the experience.
- **Golden Week:** Open and very busy.

#### Bird Land (Ginza) -- Michelin-starred yakitori
- **Address:** 4-2-15 Ginza, Chuo-ku, Tokyo (Tsukamoto Sozan Bldg B1F, 103m from Ginza Station exit C6)
- **Hours:** 17:00-21:30
- **Closed:** Mondays, Sundays, and public holidays
- **Price:** 6,000-10,000 yen/pp
- **Reservations:** Strongly recommended. Book via OpenTable or phone.
- **Payment:** Cards accepted
- **What to order:** Omakase course. Chef Toshihiro Wada uses free-range Okukuji Shamo gamecock, grilled over premium Kishu Binchotan charcoal. Every cut -- thigh, breast, liver, heart -- is treated with precision.
- **Notes:** This is elite yakitori. One Michelin star. Basement location in Ginza. This is where yakitori becomes art. Counter seats let you watch the master work.
- **Golden Week:** CLOSED Sundays and public holidays. Plan for a weeknight visit (Apr 25-28 or May 9).

#### Toriki (Yakitori hidden gem)
- **Address:** Various locations in Shibuya/Ebisu area
- **Hours:** Evening hours
- **Price:** ~3,000-5,000 yen/pp
- **What to order:** Salt-grilled chicken parts, especially the tsukune (chicken meatball). Simple, executed perfectly.
- **Notes:** Less famous than Bird Land but serious yakitori enthusiasts consider places like Toriki equally excellent. More local vibe, less formality.

### 7. IZAKAYA

#### Ebisu Yokocho
- **Address:** Near Ebisu Station (east exit, through the Atre building)
- **Hours:** Most stalls 17:00-midnight+
- **Closed:** Varies by stall
- **Price:** 2,000-4,000 yen/pp
- **Reservations:** None at most stalls
- **Payment:** Cash mostly
- **What to order:** Hop between stalls. Try the motsu-nabe (offal hot pot), yakitori, and whatever looks good. Order nama beer (draft) or highballs.
- **Notes:** This is a network of hidden alleys right near Ebisu Station. Primarily locals -- most tourists do not know about this. Lantern-lit, intimate, authentic. Perfect post-dinner drinks spot.
- **Golden Week:** Open and lively.

#### Shinpo (hidden gem izakaya)
- **Address:** Verify via Google Maps; typically in the Kagurazaka or similar local neighborhood
- **Hours:** Evening
- **Price:** ~3,000-5,000 yen/pp
- **What to order:** Creamy crab croquettes, iburigakko (smoked daikon) with cream cheese, seasonal small plates. Pair with Japanese sake or shochu.
- **Notes:** Look for the blue noren curtains. This is the kind of place where you sit at the bar, the chef talks to you (in Japanese, mostly), and the food is honest and excellent.

#### Kagurazaka area (general recommendation)
- **Address:** Kagurazaka neighborhood, near Iidabashi Station
- **Hours:** Evening
- **Price:** Varies
- **Notes:** Walking the lantern-lit alleys of Kagurazaka at dusk feels like stepping back in time. Quiet tea houses, charming izakayas. Less tourist-heavy than Shinjuku. Pop into anything that looks inviting with a noren curtain.

### 8. STREET FOOD AREAS

#### Tsukiji Outer Market (TOP PRIORITY -- Brad's #1)
- **Address:** Tsukiji, Chuo-ku, Tokyo (Tsukiji Station or Tsukijishijo Station)
- **Hours:** Most stalls 5:00am-14:00. Go in the morning (arrive by 9-10am ideally).
- **Closed:** Sundays and some Wednesdays (varies by stall)
- **Price:** Individual items 200-1,000 yen each
- **Payment:** Cash at most stalls; some accept cards
- **What to eat:** Grilled seafood skewers (scallops, squid, prawns), tamagoyaki (sweet rolled omelet -- try Yamachou or Shouro), fresh sashimi from Kimura, oysters on the half shell, uni (sea urchin), baby octopus stuffed with quail egg, and ichigo daifuku (strawberry mochi).
- **Notes:** The inner wholesale market moved to Toyosu, but the outer market is the real draw for foodies. Over 400 shops and stalls. Go early, go hungry.
- **Golden Week:** Can be extremely crowded Apr 29-May 6. Your Tokyo dates (Apr 25-28) are pre-Golden Week -- excellent timing for Tsukiji.

#### Ameyoko (Ameya-Yokocho)
- **Address:** Under the JR tracks between Ueno and Okachimachi Stations
- **Hours:** Stalls from ~10:00-19:00
- **Price:** Individual items 200-800 yen
- **Payment:** Cash mostly
- **What to eat:** Takoyaki, fresh fruit cups, chocolate-covered bananas, kebabs, mochi. More chaotic and market-like than Tsukiji.
- **Notes:** Less touristy than Tsukiji, more of a local market feel. Good to combine with Ueno area sightseeing. Lively and fun.

#### Yanaka Ginza
- **Address:** Near Nippori Station (convenient for your May 9-10 hotel at MyStays Nippori!)
- **Hours:** Shops ~10:00-18:00
- **Price:** Individual items 200-500 yen
- **What to eat:** Cat-shaped taiyaki, fuku nyan-yaki, renkon hasami-age (lotus root). Nostalgic shopping street.
- **Notes:** Old-school Tokyo charm. Your May 9-10 hotel is right here -- perfect for a morning stroll on your last day.

### 9. TONKATSU

#### Tonkatsu Narikura -- THE BEST IN TOKYO (reservation only)
- **Address:** 4-33-9 Naritahigashi, Suginami-ku, Tokyo (3 min walk from Minami-Asagaya Station, Marunouchi Line)
- **Hours:** Lunch 11:00-14:00, Dinner 17:30-20:00
- **Closed:** Check current schedule
- **Price:** ~6,000 yen/pp
- **Reservations:** REQUIRED -- completely reservation-only. Book via Omakase.in or TableCheck. Slots open one week ahead at 12:00 JST. Competition is fierce. Have your hotel concierge help.
- **Payment:** Cards likely accepted
- **What to order:** Rosu katsu (pork loin cutlet). No hint of oiliness. The coating is shatteringly crisp, the pork juicy and tender. Tabelog's #1 tonkatsu in Japan.
- **Notes:** Worth the effort to book. This will redefine what you think tonkatsu can be. Small, intimate, serious.
- **Golden Week:** May close during GW. Try for Apr 25-28.

#### Tonkatsu Sugita -- Bib Gourmand (10 consecutive years)
- **Address:** 3-8-3 Kotobuki, Taito-ku, Tokyo (7 min walk from Tawaramachi Station, 3 min from Kuramae Station exit A5)
- **Hours:** Lunch 11:30-14:30 (LO 13:30), Dinner 17:00-21:00 (LO 20:15). Closes when stock runs out.
- **Closed:** Thursdays and 3rd Wednesday
- **Price:** 3,000-4,500 yen/pp
- **Reservations:** Required. Book via Omakase JapanEatinerary, byFood, or SAVOR JAPAN. Phone: 03-3844-5529
- **Payment:** Cards accepted
- **What to order:** Rosu katsu or hire katsu (tenderloin). Thin, crispy coating -- 10 years of Bib Gourmand recognition. The panko is paper-thin and shatters beautifully.
- **Notes:** Near Asakusa -- combine with temple visit. Excellent quality at honest prices.

#### Tonkatsu Hasegawa -- Bib Gourmand
- **Address:** Ryogoku area, Tokyo
- **Hours:** Check current hours
- **Price:** ~2,000-3,000 yen/pp
- **What to order:** Their premium Hiraboku Sangenton pork from Yamagata with house-made panko breadcrumbs. Golden, light, never oily.
- **Notes:** Newer Bib Gourmand pick. Great if Narikura and Sugita are booked.

### 11. UNIQUE FOOD EXPERIENCES

#### Fileja (15-course pasta omakase)
- **Address:** 2-13-1 Kanda Sudacho, Chiyoda City, Tokyo (Akihabara area)
- **Hours:** Dinner only (check current schedule)
- **Price:** ~10,000-15,000 yen/pp
- **Reservations:** Required. Only 8 seats. One chef prepares and serves everything.
- **What to order:** The full omakase — 15 courses with 8 different handmade pasta shapes. Standouts: truffle and crab tagliatelle, lamb ragu gnocchi, braised beef cheek. Every pasta is crafted in front of you.
- **Notes:** Blown up on Instagram as one of the best pasta meals in the world. The one-chef, one-kitchen intimacy makes this unforgettable. Not Japanese food per se, but a uniquely Japanese take on Italian mastery. Book ahead.

#### Gyukatsu Motomura (beef cutlet you grill yourself)
- **Address:** Multiple locations: Shibuya (basement level near station), Shinjuku, Harajuku, Akihabara
- **Hours:** Lunch and dinner (varies by location)
- **Price:** 1,930-3,060 yen/pp
- **Reservations:** Some locations via TableCheck (Shinjuku). Others walk-in only.
- **Payment:** Cards at most locations
- **What to order:** 1.5 cutlet set (195g, 2,600 yen). Breaded beef cutlet arrives rare -- you sear it on a personal hot stone grill at your table. Dip in soy-wasabi, rock salt, or curry sauce.
- **Notes:** Uniquely interactive. The beef is tender and the DIY grilling is fun. Shibuya location near your hotel will have 30-60 min waits. Harajuku or Shinjuku may be faster.
- **Golden Week:** Expect long waits.

#### Shirohige's Cream Puff Factory (Totoro cream puffs)
- **Address:** 5-3-1 Daita, Setagaya-ku, Tokyo (near Shimokitazawa)
- **Hours:** 10:30-19:00
- **Price:** ~450-600 yen per cream puff
- **What to order:** Seasonal Totoro-shaped cream puffs (custard, chocolate, matcha fillings). Also has a cafe upstairs.
- **Notes:** Great for Gail and anyone who loves Studio Ghibli. Adorable, photogenic, genuinely delicious.

#### I'm Donut? (already on your list)
- **Address:** Multiple locations; Nakameguro is the original
- **Hours:** Opens 10:00, sells out by early afternoon
- **Price:** ~400-600 yen per donut
- **What to order:** The original brioche donut with cream filling. Fluffy, light, not too sweet.
- **Notes:** Brad loves donuts -- this is a must. Arrive early, they sell out. Also check Higuma Donuts (Hokkaido ingredients) and Mister Donut (Pon de Ring for chewy mochi texture).

#### Pizza Bar on 38th (#2 Pizza in the World)
- **Address:** Mandarin Oriental Tokyo, 38th Floor, 2-1-1 Nihonbashi Muromachi, Chuo-ku, Tokyo
- **Hours:** Lunch 11:30-14:00, Dinner 17:30-21:00
- **Price:** ~15,000-20,000 yen/pp (omakase pizza course)
- **Reservations:** ESSENTIAL. Only 8 seats. Book 2 months ahead via hotel website or phone.
- **What to order:** Omakase pizza course — the chef selects seasonal toppings and creates a progression of pizzas. Neapolitan-style in a wood-fired oven with stunning 38th-floor views.

   - OpenTable -- limited but growing in Japan
   - Your hotel concierge -- especially at Ritz-Carlton level properties (you are not staying there, but if you know someone...)
3. **Phone reservations:** Many places only take phone reservations in Japanese. Options:
   - Have your hotel front desk call for you
   - Use a concierge service
   - Walk in early before peak times

### Payment
- **Always carry cash.** Many excellent small restaurants are cash-only.
- 7-Eleven ATMs are everywhere and reliably accept foreign cards.
- Department store restaurants and chains generally accept cards.

### Timing Tips
- **Lunch is your secret weapon.** Same kitchen, same quality, 30-60% lower prices than dinner at most places. Michelin-starred lunch deals in Tokyo are the best dining hack in the world.
- **Avoid peak lunch (12:00-13:00)** at walk-in spots. Go at 11:00-11:30 or after 13:30.
- **Dinner reservations:** Book for 17:30-18:00 to get the first seating (less wait, fresher kitchen).

### Golden Week Survival
- Apr 29 (Showa Day): Many places closed
- May 3 (Constitution Day): Many places closed
- May 4 (Greenery Day): Many places closed
- May 5 (Children's Day): Many places closed
- May 6 (Observed holiday): Some places closed
- **Chain restaurants, hotel restaurants, and Dotonbori/Shinsekai stalls** generally stay open throughout.
- **Small family-run spots** may close for several days. Always confirm before going.
- Your Tokyo dates (Apr 25-28) are mostly pre-Golden Week -- take advantage and hit the harder-to-book spots then.
