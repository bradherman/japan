# Coffee, Donuts & Matcha Guide for Japan Trip

Research compiled for Brad & Alyona's April 25 - May 10, 2026 trip.
Route: Tokyo (Apr 25-30) -> Kyoto (Apr 30-May 4) -> Osaka (May 4-7) -> Hakone (May 7-9) -> Tokyo (May 9-10)

---

## PART 1: SPECIALTY COFFEE

### TOKYO (Apr 25-30 + May 9-10)

#### 1. Glitch Coffee & Roasters (HIGH PRIORITY)

The crown jewel of Tokyo's single-origin scene. Founded by Kiyokazu Suzuki, Glitch focuses exclusively on light-roast, single-origin beans roasted in-house. Every cup is hand-dripped to order. This is serious, no-shortcuts specialty coffee.

**Coffee Flight**: YES, still available. You can order a tasting flight of 2-3 different single-origin coffees, each hand-dripped, allowing you to compare origins and processing methods side by side. This is exactly what you want to do here.

**Jimbocho (Main Roastery & Cafe)**
- Address: 3-16 Kanda-Nishikicho, Chiyoda-ku, Tokyo 101-0054 (Kanamura Bldg 1F)
- Hours: Mon-Fri 7:30am-8:00pm / Sat-Sun 9:00am-7:00pm
- No holidays
- Nearest station: Jimbocho (5 min walk)

**Ginza**
- Address: VORT Ginza East III, 4-13-8 Ginza, Chuo-ku, Tokyo 104-0061
- Hours: 9:00am-6:00pm
- No holidays
- Nearest station: Higashi-Ginza

**Why it's special**: Relentlessly focused on showcasing the unique character of each bean's origin. No blends, no flavored drinks, no distractions. The flight is the best way to experience this philosophy.

**Price range**: ~Y600-1,200 per cup; flight ~Y1,500-2,000

**Recommendation**: Go to the Jimbocho main location for the full experience. It's close to Pharos Coffee, so you could do a morning coffee crawl in the Jimbocho/Kanda area.

---

#### 2. Koffee Mameya (TWO locations, both worth visiting)

**Koffee Mameya Omotesando (Bean Shop + Counter)**
- Address: 4-15-3 Jingumae, Shibuya-ku, Tokyo 150-0001
- Hours: 10:00am-6:00pm daily
- Nearest station: Omotesando (5 min walk)
- No reservations needed

A unique concept: a "coffee sommelier" experience. The baristas consult with you about your flavor preferences, then recommend and brew a cup from their rotating selection of ~25 beans sourced from 7+ roasters (4 Japanese, plus Denmark, Hong Kong, and Melbourne). It's like an omakase for beans.

**Koffee Mameya Kakeru (Omakase Coffee Course)**
- Address: 2-16-14 Hirano, Koto-ku, Tokyo (Kiyosumi-Shirakawa area)
- Hours: Mon-Fri 10:00am-5:00pm / Sat-Sun 9:00am-5:00pm
- **Reservations required** via TableCheck
- Starting at Y5,000 (~$35 USD)

This is the full omakase coffee experience: a multi-course coffee tasting at a counter, with each brew paired with confections. Seasonal varieties, cold brews, milk-based drinks, and coffee mocktails. This is for when you want coffee treated like fine wine.

**Recommendation**: Visit Omotesando for the casual "pick your bean" experience (easy to pair with Harajuku/Omotesando shopping). If you want a splurge coffee experience, book Kakeru — but be aware it's a time commitment.

---

#### 3. Streamer Coffee Company

Founded by Hiroshi Sawada, a latte art world champion. More accessible than some of the ultra-serious spots but still quality specialty coffee with excellent espresso drinks.

**Shibuya (most convenient for your hotel)**
- Address: 1-20-28 Shibuya, Shibuya-ku, Tokyo
- Hours: Mon-Fri 8:00am-6:00pm / Sat-Sun 10:00am-6:00pm
- Nearest station: Shibuya (walking distance from Granbell Hotel)

Also has locations in Harajuku, Nakameguro, Higashi-Kitazawa, and Toranomon.

**Why it's special**: Great lattes and espresso drinks. The Shibuya location is literally walking distance from your hotel, making it an ideal morning coffee stop.

**Price range**: ~Y400-700

---

#### 4. Onibus Coffee

One of Tokyo's OG specialty shops (est. 2012). Founded by Atsushi Sakao, who imports top-grade beans from Rwanda, Guatemala, and other origins, roasting them on-site. The Nakameguro location is a beautifully converted old house with a downstairs stand/roastery and upstairs seating area.

**Nakameguro (Flagship)**
- Address: 2-14-1 Kamimeguro, Meguro-ku, Tokyo 153-0051
- Hours: Mon-Fri 9:00am-6:00pm
- Nearest station: Nakameguro (8 min walk)

10 total locations across Tokyo, including Yakumo (near Komazawa Park).

**Why it's special**: Community-focused, beautiful spaces, consistently excellent light-roast pour-overs. The Nakameguro location feels like visiting someone's converted home.

**Price range**: ~Y400-800

---

#### 5. Leaves Coffee Roasters

Founded by Yasuo Ishii, a former professional boxing champion, who got into coffee after receiving a gifted bag of beans in 2010. Now recognized worldwide as one of Japan's best roasters.

- Address: 1-8-8 Honjo, Sumida-ku, Tokyo 130-0004 (Kuramae area)
- Hours: Fri-Mon only, 10:00am-5:00pm (CLOSED Tue-Thu)
- Nearest station: Kuramae or Ryogoku

**Why it's special**: Limited hours create exclusivity. The beans are exceptional, and the Kuramae neighborhood is a cool area with other craft shops and cafes worth exploring.

**Price range**: ~Y500-900

**Important**: Only open 4 days a week. Plan accordingly.

---

#### 6. Pharos Coffee

Japan's first outpost of Taipei's celebrated Pharos Coffee. Sleek, minimalist space in Jimbocho that doubles as an art gallery. Known for expertly brewed light roasts.

- Address: 1-25-4 Kanda-Jinbocho, Chiyoda-ku, Tokyo
- Hours: 11:00am-6:00pm
- Nearest station: Jimbocho

**Why it's special**: Around 5 carefully selected beans available as hand-drip, cold brew, or cold brew latte. The art gallery aspect adds visual interest. Being in Jimbocho, it pairs perfectly with a Glitch Coffee visit since they're in the same neighborhood.

**Price range**: ~Y600-1,000 for counter drinks

---

#### 7. Cafe Kitsune

A mainstay of Tokyo's third-wave scene with a recently reopened Aoyama location. Attached to the Maison Kitsune fashion brand.

- Location: Aoyama (new location, a few blocks from previous)
- Nearest station: Omotesando

**Why it's special**: Beautiful space, solid espresso, and you can browse the Maison Kitsune clothing line. Good pairing with Omotesando shopping.

**Price range**: ~Y500-900

---

#### 8. Honorable Mention: Fuglen Tokyo

Norwegian specialty coffee by day, cocktail bar by night. Located in Tomigaya (near Yoyogi Park).

- Hours: Coffee from 8:00am, cocktails from 6:00pm
- Nearest station: Yoyogi-Hachiman

Good option if you're in the Yoyogi/Shibuya area and want coffee that transitions into evening cocktails.

### KYOTO (Apr 30 - May 4)

#### 1. Weekenders Coffee Tominokoji (HIGH PRIORITY)

Arguably the best roaster in all of Kyoto and a pioneer of the city's third-wave coffee scene. Owner was inspired by Tim Wendelboe and adopted a Scandinavian-style light roasting approach. The result is clean, bright, nuanced cups.

- Address: 560 Honeyanocho, Nakagyo-ku, Kyoto 604-8064
- Hours: 7:30am-6:00pm daily, **closed Wednesday**
- Phone: +81 75-746-2206
- Nearest station: Karasuma (short walk)

**Why it's special**: Small, focused, obsessively quality-driven. This is a roaster's roaster. The space is tucked in the back of a parking lot, which adds to the "hidden gem" feel. Their light roast pour-overs are transcendent.

**Price range**: ~Y400-800

**Note**: Closed Wednesday. Your Kyoto days are Apr 30 (Thu) through May 4 (Mon), so you're good any day.

---

#### 2. Kurasu

A fascinating bridge between old kissaten culture and new specialty coffee. The founder grew up in his parents' kissaten, then spent time in the New York espresso scene and Australian third-wave culture before returning to Kyoto to create Kurasu.

**Kurasu Kyoto Stand (Main, near Kyoto Station)**
- Address: 552 Higashiaburanokoji-cho, Shimogyo-ku, Kyoto 600-8235
- Hours: 8:00am-6:00pm (check social media for closing days)
- Phone: 075-744-0804
- 5 minutes from Kyoto Station

**Kurasu Ebisugawa**
- Address: 551 Yamanakacho, Nakagyo-ku, Kyoto 604-0815
- Hours: 10:00am-6:00pm

**Why it's special**: They also retail beans from partner roasters across Japan, so you can discover new favorites. The Kyoto Stand location is convenient for your arrival day or any day you're passing through the station area.

**Price range**: ~Y400-700

---

#### 3. Iolite Coffee Roasters

A hidden gem in Nakagyo. Owner Daisuke Yoshida set up his cafe in his grandfather's old wagashiya (traditional sweets shop). The redesign is minimal and beautiful: clean cedar beams, a long bar stretching to a back nook.

- Location: Nakagyo-ku, Kyoto
- Hours: Check Instagram for current hours

**Why it's special**: The space, the story, the quality. This is the kind of under-the-radar spot that feels like a personal discovery.

---

#### 4. % Arabica Kyoto

While more well-known (and sometimes crowded), their Higashiyama location overlooking the Yasaka Pagoda is genuinely stunning.

- Locations: Higashiyama (scenic), Arashiyama, Fujii Daimaru
- Hours: Generally 8:00am-6:00pm

**Note**: Can get long lines, especially during Golden Week. Best visited early morning.

### OSAKA (May 4-7)

#### 1. Lilo Coffee Roasters (HIGH PRIORITY)

A Shinsaibashi institution. They typically have 20+ single-origin beans available at any given time, and you choose which one to have brewed. The selection is staggering for such a small shop.

**Lilo Coffee Roasters (Main)**
- Address: 1F, 1-10-28 Nishishinsaibashi, Chuo-ku, Osaka
- Hours: 11:00am-11:00pm daily
- Very close to Cross Hotel Osaka (your hotel)

**Lilo Coffee Kissa (Sister Location)**
- Address: Kaneko Bldg 2F, 2-7-25 Shinsaibashisuji, Chuo-ku, Osaka
- Hours: Weekdays 1:00pm-10:00pm / Weekends & holidays 11:00am-10:00pm

**Why it's special**: The sheer breadth of bean selection is unmatched. The late hours (until 11pm!) mean you can grab a quality pour-over even after dinner. The Kissa location has a more intimate, seated atmosphere.

**Price range**: ~Y500-1,000

**Recommendation**: Extremely convenient from your Cross Hotel. Hit the main location for the full bean selection experience.

---

#### 2. Takamura Wine & Coffee Roasters

A wine shop that also happens to be one of Osaka's best coffee roasters. The combination sounds odd but works brilliantly.

- Address: 2-2-18 Edobori, Nishi-ku, Osaka 550-0002
- Hours: 11:00am-7:30pm (last order 7:00pm), **closed Wednesday**
- Phone: +81 6-6443-3519
- Nearest station: Higobashi or Awaza

**Why it's special**: Serious roasting operation with a beautiful space. The wine + coffee combination means the staff understand flavor complexity at a deep level. Worth the short trip from Shinsaibashi.

**Price range**: ~Y500-900

**Note**: Closed Wednesday. Your Osaka days are May 4 (Mon) through May 7 (Thu), so plan around this.

---

#### 3. Mel Coffee Roasters

Melbourne-inspired specialty coffee with on-site roasting on a vintage 5kg Probat roaster. You can watch the roasting process while you drink.

**Shinmachi (Original)**
- Address: 1-20-4 Shinmachi, Nishi-ku, Osaka 550-0013
- Hours: Tue-Fri 9:00am-7:00pm / Sat-Sun 11:00am-7:00pm / **closed Monday**
- Phone: +81 6-4394-8177

**Time Out Market Osaka**
- Hours: 11:00am-11:00pm daily

**Why it's special**: Great espresso-based drinks with a Melbourne cafe vibe. The original location is intimate and focused.

**Price range**: ~Y400-800

---

#### 4. Glitch Coffee Osaka

Yes, Glitch has an Osaka outpost too. Located in Festival Tower with a dramatic open-plan seating area under the escalator, with ceiling-to-floor windows.

- Location: Festival Tower, Nakanoshima area
- Same single-origin, light-roast philosophy as Tokyo

If you loved the Tokyo Glitch flight, this gives you another shot at it in Osaka.

### HAKONE (May 7-9)

#### Hakone Roasting Coffee (Bakery & Cafe)

A multi-floor establishment on Lake Ashi with spectacular views.

- Address: 9-1 Motohakone, Hakone-machi, Ashigarashimo-gun, Kanagawa
- Phone: 0460-85-1530
- Hours:
  - 1F Bakery: 9:00am-5:00pm
  - 1F Parlor: 10:00am-5:00pm
  - 2F Cafe: 9:00am-5:00pm (last order 4:30pm)
  - 3F Coffee & Vista: 10:00am-4:00pm (last order 3:30pm)

**Why it's special**: The 3rd floor is the coffee destination: hand-dripped, house-roasted coffee with panoramic Lake Ashi views. The bakery on the 1st floor is also excellent. Given that your Hakone days are about relaxing at the ryokan with short outings, this is a perfect low-key excursion.

**Recommendation**: Go to the 3F Coffee & Vista for the full experience. Arrive before noon to avoid afternoon crowds (Golden Week will be in effect).

**Price range**: ~Y500-800

## PART 2: DONUTS

### TOKYO

#### 1. I'm Donut? (HIGH PRIORITY)

The most hyped donut in Japan. Created by pastry chef Fukuda, these are brioche-based "raw donuts" that are incredibly soft, pillowy, and slightly creamy inside. Not a traditional donut — more like a fried brioche cloud dusted with millet sugar.

**Locations & Hours:**

| Location | Address | Hours |
|----------|---------|-------|
| Nakameguro | 1-22-10 Kamimeguro, Meguro-ku | 11:00am-7:00pm (or sold out) |
| Shibuya | 2-9-1 Shibuya, Shibuya-ku | 11:00am-8:00pm |
| Harajuku | Jingumae area | ~11:00am-7:00pm |
| Omotesando (Gluten-Free) | Along Route 246 | ~11:00am-7:00pm |
| Jiyugaoka | 2-11-3 Jiyugaoka, Meguro-ku | 9:00am-8:00pm |

**Wait Times:**
- Weekday ~noon: ~5 minutes
- Weekday afternoon (rain): ~10 minutes
- Weekend: Up to 1 hour
- **Golden Week warning**: Your Tokyo dates overlap with Golden Week start (Apr 29). Expect longer lines than normal, especially Apr 29-30.

**What to Order:**
- **Plain raw donut** (Y237) — the signature, start here
- **Matcha raw donut** — rivals the plain in popularity
- **Rare Donut Chocolate** (Jiyugaoka exclusive)
- **Rare Donut Custard** (Jiyugaoka exclusive)
- The Shibuya location has up to 80 different varieties

**Strategy**: Hit Shibuya (closest to your hotel) on a weekday morning right at 11am. The Shibuya location is the largest with the most variety. Alternatively, Jiyugaoka opens at 9am and has exclusive flavors.

**Price range**: ~Y237-500 per donut

---

#### 2. Higuma Doughnuts

Uses all-natural Hokkaido ingredients, no additives or preservatives. The result is fluffy, springy doughnuts that taste clean and honest.

**Gakugei-Daigaku (Main Store)**
- Address: 2-8-21 Takaban, Meguro-ku, Tokyo 152-0004
- Hours: Mon-Tue, Thu-Sat 10:00am-9:00pm / Sun 10:00am-9:00pm / **closed Wednesday**

**Omotesando (Higuma Doughnuts x Coffee Wrights)**
- Address: 4-9-13 Jingumae, Shibuya-ku, Tokyo 150-0001 (Minagawa Village)
- Hours: Weekdays 11:00am-6:00pm / Weekends from 10:00am / **closed Wednesday**

**What to Order:**
- **Plain** — the base test of any donut shop
- **Kinako** (roasted soybean powder) — uniquely Japanese
- **Cinnamon sugar** — classic done well
- **Black sugar** — Okinawan brown sugar
- **Seasonal specials**: honey-mascarpone, limoncello, raspberry jam

**Recommendation**: The Omotesando location is the most convenient — it's a collaboration space with Coffee Wrights (great specialty coffee), so you get excellent donuts AND coffee in one stop. Pairs well with a Koffee Mameya Omotesando visit and general Harajuku/Omotesando exploring.

**Price range**: ~Y250-400 per donut

---

#### 3. Mister Donut — Pon de Ring

**Is it worth trying?** YES. This is not fine pastry, but the Pon de Ring is genuinely unique and worth experiencing. It's a ring of connected chewy, mochi-like dough balls — texturally unlike any donut you've had. At Y130-200 (~$0.90-1.35), it costs almost nothing.

**Locations**: Everywhere. There are Mister Donut shops in virtually every major train station and shopping area in Japan. You'll walk past one without trying.

**What to Order:**
- **Pon de Ring (plain)** — the signature, chewy mochi texture
- **Pon de Ring Matcha** — Uji matcha version
- **Pon de Ring Brown Sugar** — seasonal
- Check for seasonal/collaboration flavors

**Strategy**: Don't plan a special trip. Just grab one when you spot a Mister Donut near a train station. It's a Y150 snack, not a destination.

**Price range**: Y130-200

---

## PART 3: MATCHA EXPERIENCES (Kyoto Focus)

### Tea Ceremony Experiences

#### 1. Ippodo Tea — Kyoto Main Store (TOP RECOMMENDATION)

In business since 1717 (over 300 years). This is the real deal for matcha. The main store on historic Teramachi Street has a tearoom where you can learn to whisk your own matcha, paired with a seasonal Japanese sweet.

- Address: Teramachi-dori, Nijo-agaru, Nakagyo-ku, Kyoto
- Store hours: 9:00am-6:00pm daily
- Cafe hours: 10:00am-5:00pm (last order 4:30pm), **closed every 2nd Wednesday**
- 5-minute walk from Kamo River and Kyoto Imperial Palace

**Experience**: Sit at the semi-circular counter with a tea kettle, whisk your own matcha while a staff member guides you, enjoy with traditional sweets. You can also sample and purchase their teas to bring home.

**Why it's the best**: This isn't a tourist tea ceremony — it's a working tea shop where you learn the actual technique from people who've been doing this for three centuries. Affordable, authentic, and you can buy incredible matcha to bring home.

**Price range**: ~Y700-1,500 for the cafe experience; matcha tins for home Y1,000-5,000+

---

#### 2. Tea Ceremony Nagomi

A more structured, English-friendly tea ceremony experience using matcha from Gion Tsujiri (150+ year history).

- Website: kyoto-nagomi-tea.com
- Location: Central Kyoto
- **Reservations recommended**

**Experience**: Learn the 400-year history of tea ceremony, make your own matcha, enjoy with traditional Japanese sweets. Good for a first-timer wanting the cultural context explained.

---

#### 3. Marukyu Koyamaen — Nishinotoin Tea Shop & Motoan Tea House

A 300-year-old tea producer from Uji. Their Kyoto shop has a beautiful machiya-housed cafe (Sabo Motoan) offering matcha, gyokuro, and sencha with traditional sweets.

- Address: 561 Nishinotoin-cho, Sanbo, Oike-sagaru, Nishinotoin-dori, Nakagyo-ku, Kyoto
- Shop hours: 9:30am-6:00pm
- Cafe hours: 10:30am-5:00pm
- **Closed Wednesday**
- Phone: 075-223-0909

**Price range**: ~Y800-1,500 for tea + sweet sets

---

### Matcha Dessert Cafes

#### 1. Assemblages Kakimoto (TOP MATCHA DESSERT)

An award-winning patisserie by celebrated pastry chef/chocolatier Akihiro Kakimoto. Their "The Vert" matcha parfait is a work of art.

- Address: 587-5 Matsumotocho, Nakagyo-ku, Kyoto 604-0982
- Cafe hours: 12:00pm-5:00pm
- Store hours: 12:00pm-7:00pm
- Dinner: By reservation from 6:00pm
- Open Mon, Thu-Sun / **closed Tue-Wed**
- **Reservations strongly recommended for dine-in**

**The Vert Parfait**: Layers of matcha espuma, ganache, meringue, pistachio cream, tonka bean ice cream, and mochi. Made with premium matcha from Okawa Farm in Ujitawara. Looks like a moss garden. Extraordinary.

**Price range**: ~Y1,500-2,500 for desserts

---

#### 2. Kyo Hayashiya

Classic Kyoto matcha cafe using three different preparations of matcha in their signature parfait: jelly, cream, and ice cream, garnished with shiratama dumplings, whipped cream, and wafer.

- Multiple locations in Kyoto
- No reservation needed for cafe

**Price range**: ~Y1,000-1,800

---

#### 3. Zen Cafe (Kagizen Yoshifusa)

Famous for "Yuuzen" — a deconstructed matcha parfait served on a tray with cubes of matcha castella, vanilla and green tea ice cream, butter shavings, red bean paste, cream, mini monaka wafers, and kuromitsu syrup.

- Location: Gion area, Kyoto
- Great for a Gion walking day

---

### Day Trip: Uji (Matcha Origin Town)

Uji is only ~20 minutes by train from Kyoto and is the birthplace of Japan's finest matcha. If you have a half-day free, it's worth the trip for the most authentic matcha experience possible.

#### Nakamura Tokichi Honten

Founded 1854. A tea house in a converted tea factory.

- Location: Central Uji, short walk from JR Uji Station
- **Must-order**: "Namacha Jelly (Matcha)" — smooth, silky matcha jelly with adzuki beans and shiratama
- **Main store exclusive**: "Maruto Parfait (Matcha)" in a bamboo tube — namacha jelly, matcha castella, matcha ice cream, special whipped cream

**Warning**: Lines can be very long, especially during Golden Week. Arrive early or be prepared to wait 30-60+ minutes.

#### Tsujirihei Honten (Tsujiri Uji Main Shop)

160+ years of tea tradition. The Uji main store has an in-store teahouse with a Kyoto-style garden.

- Location: Minutes from JR Uji Station
- **Must-order**: "Matcha ni Somaru Parfait" — matcha ice cream, shiratama mochi, red bean paste, jelly
