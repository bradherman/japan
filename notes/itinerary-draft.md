## Day 1: Friday, April 25 — Tokyo — Arrival / Shibuya

*With Dave & Gail. You land at 3pm, clear customs ~4pm, N'EX to Shibuya ~6pm. Tonight is about shaking off jet lag with a low-key first dinner.*

**Morning Coffee:** N/A (in-flight)

### Afternoon
- **3:00 PM** — Land at Narita. Clear immigration/customs (~45-60 min)
- **~4:30 PM** — Board Narita Express (N'EX) to Shibuya (direct, ~75 min, 3,250 yen/pp)
- **~6:00 PM** — Arrive Shibuya Station. Walk 5-10 min to Shibuya Granbell Hotel. Check in, drop bags, freshen up

### Dinner — Yakitori / Street Food (~7:30 PM)
1. **Omoide Yokocho (Memory Lane)** — Yakitori alley — cash only, ~1,000-2,500 yen/pp — Dozens of tiny stalls west of Shinjuku Station. Squeeze in at a counter, order chicken skewers and highballs. Perfect casual first night. *Tip: Yasubee stall is foreigner-friendly; wander deeper for more local vibes. 7 min train from Shibuya or ~15 min taxi.*
2. **Fuunji** — Tsukemen (dipping ramen) — ~1,200-1,450 yen/pp — Only 5 min walk from your hotel. Incredible dipping noodles if the group is too tired for Shinjuku. Cash only, vending machine order.
3. **Gyukatsu Motomura Shibuya** — Beef cutlet — ~2,000-2,600 yen/pp — DIY grilling at your table. Fun, interactive, close to hotel. Expect 30-60 min wait.

### Evening
- **Grandfather's** (Shibuya) — Below the streets of Shibuya, a listening bar since 1971. Owner Toru Ishikawa has curated 2,000+ albums of 70s/80s Billboard hits. Vintage sound system, dark wood, dim lights, Suntory highball. This is the source of Japan's listening bar culture. Walking distance from hotel. *Perfect low-key first night — sit, soak in the music, decompress from the flight.*
- **Nonbei Yokocho** (Shibuya) — Tiny bar alley right near the hotel. Grab a nightcap at one of the pint-sized standing bars. No cover, just point and order.
- Or crash early. You just flew across the Pacific. No shame.

### Logistics
- At Narita: Buy physical Suica card OR set up Apple Wallet Suica before landing (load 3,000-5,000 yen)
- N'EX one-way ticket: buy at JR counter in arrivals
- Carry cash tonight — most Omoide Yokocho stalls and Fuunji are cash-only
- 7-Eleven ATMs accept foreign cards

## Day 2: Saturday, April 26 — Tokyo — Tsukiji / Ginza / Shibuya Sky

*With Dave & Gail. Big first full day: morning market crawl, Ginza shopping, sunset views. Pre-Golden Week — take advantage of smaller crowds.*

**Morning Coffee:** Streamer Coffee Shibuya (walking distance from hotel, opens 10am Sat — grab on the way out OR get station coffee pre-Tsukiji)

### Morning — Tsukiji Outer Market (arrive ~7:30-8:00 AM)
- Take Ginza Line from Shibuya to Tsukiji (~20 min, 210 yen)
- **Tsukiji Outer Market food crawl** — 2-3 hours of eating your way through 400+ stalls
  - Must-eat: tamagoyaki (Shouro or Yamachou), grilled scallops (Kakigoya), fresh sashimi, uni, ichigo daifuku (Soratsuki), tuna skewers + beer
  - Cash at most stalls. Closed Wed/Sun — Saturday is great
  - *Optional knife stop:* Masamoto knives (since 1850s) at the market edge
- Done by ~10:30 AM

### Late Morning — Ginza (walk from Tsukiji, ~15 min)
- **Seiko Museum Ginza** — Free admission, reservation required. 6 floors of horology. Grand Seiko Museum on 6th floor. Allow 1-1.5 hrs. *(Book online at museum.seiko.co.jp)*
- **Grand Seiko Flagship Boutique Ginza Namiki** — 2 min walk from museum. Browse/buy Grand Seiko. Tax-free with passport. Allow 1-2 hrs.
- **Chopstick Making Workshop (Ginza Hashikura)** — ~30-60 min, ~$27/pp + premium wood. Book for all 4. Take home same day. *(Book via GetYourGuide or Klook)*

### Lunch (2-3 options) — in Ginza area, ~1:00-2:00 PM
*Cuisine: Street food from Tsukiji was breakfast/brunch — lunch should be something different*
1. **Nakajima** — Michelin-starred sardine set lunch — 990 yen/pp (!!) — Shinjuku, but only a 15 min subway. Cheapest Michelin meal in Tokyo. Closed Sun, so today works. Queue before 11:30. *If you're still full from Tsukiji, skip formal lunch and graze.*
2. **Mugi to Olive** — Elegant clam shio ramen — ~1,200 yen/pp — Right in Ginza. Light, consomme-style broth. Closed Sun.
3. **Ginza Six basement depachika** — Gourmet food floor grazing — varied prices — Great if Tsukiji filled you up and you just want bites.

### Afternoon — Shibuya Sky (~4:30-5:00 PM arrival for sunset)
- Return to Shibuya area (~15 min from Ginza)
- **Shibuya Sky** — Book the ~17:30 sunset slot online in advance (3,400 yen for sunset slot). Watch golden hour transition to city lights from 229m above Shibuya Crossing. Allow 60-90 min. Rooftop bar (Paradise Lounge) for drinks with the view.
- **Shibuya Crossing** — You're literally above it. Watch from the sky, then experience it at street level after.

### Dinner (~7:30-8:00 PM) — Tonkatsu
1. **Tonkatsu Narikura** — Best tonkatsu in Tokyo — ~6,000 yen/pp — Reservation ONLY via Omakase.in or TableCheck. Slots open 1 week ahead at noon JST. *(If booked: take Marunouchi Line to Minami-Asagaya, 3 min walk)*
2. **Tonkatsu Sugita** — Bib Gourmand, 10 consecutive years — ~3,000-4,500 yen/pp — Near Asakusa. Book via Omakase or byFood. Great if you want to save Narikura for a weekday.
3. **Gyukatsu Motomura Shibuya** — Beef cutlet — ~2,600 yen/pp — If tonkatsu reservations fell through, this is a reliable walk-in near hotel.

### Evening
- **JBS Vinyl Bar** (Shibuya) — No cover, open 2pm-11pm. Wall of vinyl, great sound system, intimate vibe. Walking distance from hotel. *This is your spot.*
- **SG Club** (Shibuya) — Speakeasy cocktails. Two floors: casual downstairs (Guzzle), refined upstairs (Sip). Walk from JBS.

### Logistics
- Golden Week starts Apr 29 — today and tomorrow are your best days for harder-to-book restaurants
- Tonkatsu Narikura: set alarm for Saturday 9am to book next week's TableCheck slots (or try Omakase.in NOW)
- Pre-book Shibuya Sky sunset slot online ASAP
- Seiko Museum needs online reservation — do it today if not already done

### Dinner (~6:30-7:30 PM) — Teppanyaki
1. **Kisentei** — Tokyo Midtown, Roppongi — Wagyu teppanyaki lunch from 8,000-10,000 yen/pp. Private rooms, counter seats watching the chef. *(Actually better as a lunch — if you did sushi for lunch, do teppanyaki dinner)*

3. **Ebisu Yokocho** — Izakaya alley near Ebisu Station — ~2,000-4,000 yen/pp — Casual alternative. Multiple stalls, locals-heavy, great vibe. Perfect if teppanyaki feels like too much after a big sushi lunch.

## Day 4: Monday, April 28 — Tokyo — Jimbocho / Asakusa / Kappabashi

*With Dave & Gail. Coffee flight at Glitch, knife shopping in Kappabashi, temple culture in Asakusa. Last full weekday before Golden Week — hit the weekday-only spots.*

**Morning Coffee:** Glitch Coffee Jimbocho — THE coffee destination. Order the 2-3 bean tasting flight (~1,500-2,000 yen). Hand-dripped, single-origin, light-roast perfection. Opens 7:30am weekdays.

### Morning — Jimbocho + Kappabashi (~8:00-11:30 AM)
- **Glitch Coffee flight** — Allow 45-60 min to savor
- **Udon Maruka** (Jimbocho area) — Best udon in Tokyo. WEEKDAYS ONLY, closed holidays. Queue by 10:00-10:30 for 11am opening. Kamaage udon, 500-800 yen. Cash only. *This closes during Golden Week — today is your shot.*
- Walk/subway to **Kappabashi Street** (Kitchen Town) — ~20 min from Jimbocho
  - **Tsubaya** — Widest selection, handles most knives freely. Est. 1956.
  - **Kamata Hakensha** — Master sharpener, every knife gets a perfect edge
  - Allow 1-1.5 hours for knife shopping

### Lunch (~12:30-1:30 PM) — Ramen
1. **Ginza Hachigo** — Michelin Bib Gourmand ramen — ~1,200 yen/pp — Book via TableCheck (slots open Sat 9am for following week, 500 yen booking fee). The truffle-foie gras ravioli ramen. Unlike any ramen you've had. *If you booked Saturday morning, your reservation should be today or this week.*
2. **Nakiryu** — Legendary tantanmen — ~1,200 yen/pp — Arrive by 10:30am for walk-in lunch. Cash only. Closed Tue. *If you didn't queue for Maruka, queue here instead.*
3. **Konjiki Hototogisu** — 60-ingredient broth — ~1,200 yen/pp — Near Shinjuku Gyoen. Walk-in with queue. Cash only.

### Afternoon — Asakusa (~2:30-5:00 PM)
- Subway from Kappabashi to Asakusa (~5 min walk, same neighborhood)
- **Sensoji Temple** — Tokyo's oldest temple, free. Thunder Gate (Kaminarimon), Nakamise shopping street, the temple itself. Allow 1-1.5 hrs.
- **Briskstand Asakusa** — Wagyu burger if you need a snack
- Optional: **Tonkatsu Sugita** is a 7-min walk from here if you skipped it Saturday

### Dinner (~7:00 PM) — Izakaya / Yakitori
*Dave has celiac — avoid heavy gluten spots. Yakitori with salt (shio) and izakaya grilled/raw items are naturally GF. Ask for no soy sauce or request tamari.*
1. **Shinpo** — Hidden gem izakaya — ~3,000-5,000 yen/pp — Creamy crab croquettes, smoked daikon with cream cheese. Blue noren curtains. Kagurazaka area. *Many grilled/raw items are naturally GF.*
2. **Kagurazaka area** — Walk the lantern-lit alleys near Iidabashi Station. Pop into any inviting izakaya with a noren curtain. Less touristy than Shinjuku. *Izakaya menus have plenty of GF options: sashimi, grilled fish, yakitori with salt, edamame.*
3. **Toriki** — Serious yakitori, Shibuya/Ebisu — ~3,000-5,000 yen — Salt-grilled skewers (shio, not tare which has soy/wheat). Excellent tsukune. Local vibe, naturally celiac-friendly.

### Evening
- **Jazz Bar Samurai** (Shinjuku 3-chome, 5F) — Vinyl jazz bar since 1979. 2,500+ maneki-neko (lucky cats) covering every surface. Run by a husband-and-wife team. Owner Miyazaki-san curated the collection from his time in 1970s New York. Drinks ~¥700. *This is unlike any bar you've ever been to. Don't skip.*
- **Bar Benfiddich** (Shinjuku) — Book ahead. Craft cocktails with foraged ingredients. Tiny, famous, unforgettable.
- **Shinjuku Pit Inn** — Legendary jazz club, check tonight's lineup
- **Koenji UFO Club** — Psych rock venue! Check their schedule — this is the Kikagaku Moyo adjacent scene. ~20 min from Shinjuku on Chuo Line.

### Logistics
- **Bird Land closed Mon AND holidays** — moved to Day 15 (Sat May 9) when it's definitely open.
- **Dave's celiac:** Tonight's options are all GF-friendly. For yakitori, always ask for shio (salt) instead of tare (sauce contains wheat). Sashimi, grilled meats, and edamame are safe. Be cautious with tempura, gyoza, ramen, udon, and anything with soy sauce (contains wheat). Tamari is the GF alternative.
- Udon Maruka WILL close Apr 29 - May 6 (holidays). Today is your only chance.
- Arrange takkyubin luggage forwarding at hotel front desk this evening (send tomorrow morning, arrives Kyoto Apr 30)
- Tomorrow is Showa Day (Apr 29) — many small restaurants closed. Plan accordingly.

## Day 7: Thursday, May 1 — Kyoto — Fushimi Inari / Gion / Higashiyama

*Peak temple and culture day. Early start for empty torii gates. Kamogawa Odori geisha dance runs May 1-3 — tonight is your first chance.*

**Morning Coffee:** Weekenders Coffee Tominokoji — THE Kyoto coffee destination. Scandinavian-style light roast. Opens 7:30am. Closed Wed (today is Thu, perfect). Tucked behind a parking lot — hidden gem vibes.

### Morning — Fushimi Inari (~6:00-7:30 AM)
- Take Kyoto subway to Kyoto Station, then JR Nara Line 1 stop to Inari Station (shrine is literally outside the exit). Total ~25 min, ~410 yen.
- **Fushimi Inari** — Before 7am for near-empty torii gates. The iconic vermillion tunnels. Free, open 24 hrs.
- **Brad:** First section (Senbon Torii) is 15-20 min of flat/gentle walking — that's the money shot. No need to summit.
- **Fushimi Sake District** — Before heading back, walk 10 min south from Inari into the historic sake brewing district. Century-old breweries using Fushimi's famous pure spring water. Get a flight of 18 local sakes at one of the tasting rooms (~1,000-1,500 yen). *You're already here — this is the natural extension of the morning.*
- Return to hotel by ~9:30 AM for coffee

### Late Morning — Gion / Higashiyama (~10:00 AM-12:30 PM)
- Walk from hotel east along Shijo-dori into Gion
- **Yasaka Shrine** — Free, always open. Beautiful stone torii.
- Walk south through **Ninenzaka and Sannenzaka** — Stone-paved lanes, traditional shops, tea houses
- **Kiyomizu-dera** — UNESCO World Heritage. Famous wooden stage with city views. ~400 yen. Note: involves uphill walk through Higashiyama (steady but not steep — Brad should be fine at a relaxed pace).
- **% Arabica Higashiyama** — Coffee with Yasaka Pagoda views. Can have long GW lines — skip if crowded.

### Lunch (~1:00 PM) — Tempura
1. **Kitenya** — Bib Gourmand tempura — ~2,000-4,000 yen — Central location. Light batter, seasonal ingredients. Walk-in friendly.
2. **Yoshikawa Tempura** — Machiya with koi pond garden — ~5,000-8,000 yen lunch — Counter seats to watch the chef. More upscale. Reserve ahead.
3. **Tenyu** — Tempura rice bowl — ~3,000-5,000 yen — Seatings at 11:30 and 13:00. Closed Tue (today is Thu, fine). Reserve.
