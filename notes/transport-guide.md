## 1. IC Cards / Suica/Pasmo

### What Are They?
IC cards (Suica, Pasmo, ICOCA) are rechargeable smart cards used for trains, subways, buses, and convenience store purchases across Japan. They all work interchangeably nationwide -- you can use a Suica in Osaka or an ICOCA in Tokyo.

### Which Card to Get
**Suica** is the simplest choice. It works everywhere and is issued by JR East.

### Physical Suica Cards
Physical Suica cards are available again as of March 2025 (they were unavailable for ~2 years due to a chip shortage). You can buy one at:
- **Narita Airport** -- JR East ticket counters or vending machines in the arrivals area
- Any JR East station ticket machine

Cost: **500 yen deposit** (refundable when you return the card) + whatever amount you load.

### Apple Wallet Suica (Recommended)
The best option for convenience:
1. Open Wallet app on iPhone
2. Tap "+" > "Transit Card" > "Suica"
3. Choose an amount to load (minimum 1,000 yen)
4. Confirm with Face ID / Touch ID
5. Done -- tap your phone at any gate

**Benefits:**
- No deposit needed
- Reload anytime from your credit card
- Check balance on your phone
- Works on Apple Watch too
- No need to carry a physical card

**Welcome Suica App** (alternative): JR East's app specifically for tourists, 180-day validity. Works similarly to Apple Wallet Suica.

### Loading Money
- **Apple Wallet**: Add funds via linked credit/debit card (Visa, Mastercard, Amex supported)
- **Physical card**: Use fare adjustment machines or ticket machines at any station; 7-Eleven ATMs
- **Recommended starting balance**: 3,000-5,000 yen per person

### Pro Tips
- IC cards cover almost all local transit (trains, subways, buses) but NOT shinkansen or limited express trains
- You can use IC cards at convenience stores, vending machines, coin lockers, and many restaurants
- If your balance is too low for a fare, use the fare adjustment machine before exiting the gate

### Verdict: SKIP the JR Pass

**The JR Pass is not worth it for this trip.** At current prices:

**Recommendation: Buy individual tickets and keep the Nozomi 23 booking.**

## 14. Golden Week Warnings

### Dates: April 29 - May 6, 2026
Your trip overlaps significantly with Golden Week. Here is what to expect:

### Train Impacts
- **Shinkansen Nozomi trains operate with ALL reserved seats during Golden Week** -- no unreserved seating available on Nozomi. Your Apr 30 Nozomi 23 is already reserved, so this is fine.
- **Hikari and Kodama trains still have unreserved cars**, but they will be very crowded. Free-seating cars can have standing-room-only conditions.
- **Local trains and subways** will be busier than normal but manageable (Tokyo residents leave the city for Golden Week, partially offsetting tourist crowds).

### Reservations
- **Book the Osaka > Odawara Hikari (May 7) ASAP.** This is the end of Golden Week and people will be returning to Tokyo. Reserved seats sell out.
- **Book all shinkansen with reserved seats.** The extra 500-900 yen for a reservation is absolutely worth it during Golden Week.
- Shinkansen tickets can be booked via **SmartEX app** up to 1 month in advance. Mark your calendar for **April 7** to book the May 7 Hikari.

### Crowd Strategies
- **May 4 (Kyoto > Osaka):** End of Golden Week approaching. Regular trains (not shinkansen) should be fine -- just avoid unreserved shinkansen.
- **May 7 (Osaka > Hakone):** Possible return rush toward Tokyo. Book reserved seats.
- **May 9 (Hakone > Tokyo):** Golden Week is over. Should be much calmer.
- **Buses in Kyoto** will be especially bad during Golden Week (Apr 30 - May 4). Walk or use the subway when possible.
- **Popular attractions** (Fushimi Inari, Kinkaku-ji, Arashiyama) will have long queues. Go early morning (before 8 AM) or late afternoon.

### Service Changes
- Generally, trains run on **holiday schedules** during Golden Week -- slightly reduced frequency on some lines but no major disruptions
- Check for any maintenance work on specific lines closer to the trip date

## 15. Luggage Forwarding (Takkyubin)

### What Is It?
Takkyubin (literally "express delivery") is Japan's door-to-door luggage forwarding service. Instead of dragging suitcases on trains, you send them ahead to your next hotel. The main provider is **Yamato Transport** (look for the black cat logo -- "Kuroneko Yamato").

### How It Works
1. Go to your **hotel front desk** and ask for "takkyubin" or luggage forwarding
2. Fill out the shipping form (hotel staff will help -- destination hotel name and address)
3. Pay at the front desk
4. Your luggage arrives at the next hotel, typically **next day by early afternoon**

### Where to Arrange
- **Hotel front desk** (easiest -- almost all hotels in Japan offer this)
- **Convenience stores** (7-Eleven, FamilyMart, Lawson) -- they have Yamato forms
- **Yamato Transport counters** at major train stations
- **Airport Yamato counters** in Narita arrivals

### Cost Estimates

| Route | Size | Estimated Cost |
|-------|------|---------------|
| Tokyo hotel > Kyoto hotel | Large suitcase (140-160cm) | ~2,400-3,100 yen (~$15-20) |
| Kyoto hotel > Osaka hotel | Large suitcase | ~1,800-2,200 yen (~$12-14) |
| Osaka hotel > Hakone ryokan | Large suitcase | ~2,400-2,800 yen (~$15-18) |
| Hakone ryokan > Nippori hotel | Large suitcase | ~2,200-2,600 yen (~$14-17) |

Size is measured by total dimensions (length + width + height in cm). A standard large suitcase is typically 140-160cm total.

### Recommended Forwarding Plan

| When to Send | From > To | Notes |
|-------------|-----------|-------|
| Apr 29 (day before) | Shibuya Granbell > Hotel Forza Kyoto | Send morning of Apr 29, arrives Apr 30 |
| May 3 (day before) | Hotel Forza Kyoto > Cross Hotel Osaka | Send morning of May 3, arrives May 4 |
| May 6 (day before) | Cross Hotel Osaka > Hakone Gora Byakudan | Send morning of May 6, arrives May 7 |

**Total forwarding cost estimate: ~9,000-11,000 yen (~$58-71) per suitcase for all 4 legs.**

### Tips
- **Send luggage the morning BEFORE your travel day.** It arrives the next day (same-day is possible within some metro areas but next-day is standard).
- Keep a small bag/backpack with essentials for the travel day (change of clothes, toiletries, medications, chargers).
- You can also send **souvenirs and shopping bags** via takkyubin -- don't carry bags of stuff from city to city.
- **Confirm with each hotel** that they accept takkyubin deliveries (almost all do, but worth confirming).
- **Airport takkyubin on final day:** You can send luggage from Hotel MyStays Nippori to Narita Airport, but this requires 2+ days advance and you need to pick up at the airport. For the final leg, just bring your bags on the Skyliner (36 min, very manageable).

## 16. Booking Tools & Apps

### SmartEX (Shinkansen Booking)
- **What:** Official app for booking Tokaido/Sanyo/Kyushu Shinkansen tickets
- **Why:** Book reserved seats, choose your specific seat, get ~200 yen discount vs station purchase
- **How:** Download app > register with email + credit card > search and book
- **Important:** Can book up to 1 month in advance
- **Covers:** Tokyo-Kyoto, Osaka-Odawara, Odawara-Tokyo (all your shinkansen rides)
- **Tip:** You can tap in with your phone (linked to your IC card or SmartEX account) instead of using a paper ticket

### Google Maps
- Works excellently in Japan for train/subway/bus directions
- Shows real-time departures, costs, transfer info
- Set language to English -- it handles Japanese station names well

### Navitime / Yahoo Transit Japan
- Alternative transit apps popular in Japan
- Can be helpful for bus schedules that Google Maps sometimes misses

### Hyperdia (discontinued)
- No longer available. Use Google Maps or Navitime instead.
