# Nightlife Guide: Tokyo, Kyoto & Osaka

Research compiled for the Apr 25 – May 10, 2026 trip. Focused on vinyl/listening bars, jazz bars, craft cocktails, live music, and late-night neighborhoods. Emphasis on hidden gems over tourist traps.

---

## TOKYO (Apr 25–30, staying in Shibuya)

### Vinyl / Listening Bars

#### 1. JBS Jazz Bar (Shibuya) — TOP PICK
- **Address:** 1-17-10 Dogenzaka, Shibuya-ku, Tokyo
- **Hours:** Daily 2pm–11pm
- **Cover:** No cover charge (rare for Tokyo listening bars)
- **Drinks:** ~500 yen — includes Japanese whiskies (Suntory, Nikka single malts)
- **Vibe:** Intimate vinyl sanctuary tucked into Shibuya's Dogenzaka. Over 10,000 records, primarily jazz. Warm analog sound. The owner serves tea and coffee during the day, beer and spirits at night. Feels like being in someone's personal collection. Very local crowd.
- **Why go:** No cover, walking distance from Shibuya Granbell Hotel, massive jazz vinyl collection, and it opens at 2pm so it works for afternoon listening sessions too.

#### 2. Grandfather's (Shibuya) — TOP PICK — INSTAGRAM REC
- **Address:** Below street level in Shibuya (exact address: confirm via Google Maps "Grandfather's Shibuya listening bar")
- **Hours:** Evening (~7pm onward, check current hours)
- **Cover:** Likely small table charge
- **Vibe:** Underground listening bar since 1971. Owner Toru Ishikawa curated 2,000+ albums — specifically Billboard hits of the 70s and 80s. Dark wood, dim lights, faint cigarette smoke. Vintage sound system. Cold Suntory highball. This is described as "the source" of Japan's listening bar culture — the bar that inspired listening bars worldwide.
- **Why go:** 50+ years of history. Walking distance from your hotel. The most authentic listening bar experience in Tokyo. One Instagram creator who visits listening bars globally said "for the first time, it feels like I've reached the source." This is where the movement started.

#### 3. Record Bar Analog (Shibuya) — TOP PICK
- **Address:** 2-20-9 Dogenzaka, Ryuko Bldg 3F, Shibuya-ku (in the Hyakkendana district)
- **Hours:** Daily 7pm–3am
- **Cover:** 1,000 yen table charge
- **Vibe:** Hidden on the 3rd floor of a building in Shibuya's Hyakkendana (a grid of narrow alleys full of bars). Retro-chic with dim lighting and vintage decor. The sound system is a vintage Altec speaker and amplifier with a Garrard 301 turntable (developed in the 1950s for BBC). Browse the record racks freely and take your pick to the counter with the pin badge from your table to request a play.
- **Collection:** Classic rock, jazz, indie, electronic, hip-hop, funk
- **Why go:** Interactive — you choose the records. Late hours (until 3am). Walk-in only, no reservations.

#### 3. Ginza Music Bar (Ginza)
- **Address:** 7-8-13 Ginza, Chuo-ku, Tokyo
- **Hours:** Mon–Sat 7pm–4am, Closed Sunday
- **Cover:** Reportedly around 2,000–3,000 yen
- **Vibe:** Plush royal-blue seating, polished wood surfaces, impressive Tannoy speakers. A side project of DJ Shinichi Osawa and coffee producer Nobuhiro Toriba. 3,000 vinyl records and a serious DJ setup. The crowd skews sophisticated; this is a destination bar.
- **Why go:** Late hours (until 4am), world-class sound system, beautifully designed space.

#### 4. Little Soul Cafe (Shimokitazawa)
- **Address:** Shimokitazawa (exact address best confirmed via Google Maps — it is small and easy to miss)
- **Hours:** Opens 9pm nightly
- **Cover:** Minimal or none (drink minimum)
- **Vibe:** A Shimokitazawa institution since 1999. Narrow, corridor-like space crammed with over 15,000 vinyl records. Warm orange lighting. The focus is soul, funk, jazz, and dance music. The owner curates the selections with deep knowledge. It feels like drinking inside a record collection.
- **Why go:** 15,000+ records focused on soul/funk. Pairs well with an evening exploring Shimokitazawa's bohemian bar scene.

#### 5. Record Bar 33 1/3rpm (Shibuya)
- **Address:** Near Shibuya Station (Dogenzaka area)
- **Hours:** Evening hours (confirm via website: 33-1-3rpm.com)
- **Cover:** 1,540 yen per person
- **Vibe:** Opened in 2013, designed specifically around analog music and alcohol. 6,000 records covering mainly UK/US rock and pop. Clean, modern interior with good seating.
- **Why go:** Strong rock and pop collection (complements the jazz-heavy JBS).

#### 6. The Music Bar — Cave Shibuya
- **Address:** Shibuya (relocated from Yoyogi in 2021)
- **Hours:** Evening hours
- **Cover:** Check on arrival
- **Vibe:** Over 3,000 hand-selected vinyls curated by musician Takeshi Kobayashi. Fancy cocktails, spinny bar stools, plush setting. Records lean toward easy listening classics with soul, funk, and disco.
- **Why go:** More upscale listening bar experience, good cocktails alongside the vinyl.

#### 7. Bar Martha (Ebisu)
- **Address:** Ebisu area (small, unmarked — best to look up exact address on Google Maps before going)
- **Hours:** Evening
- **Cover:** Check on arrival
- **Vibe:** One of the most revered listening bars in Tokyo. Intimate, audiophile-grade setup. Quiet, focused atmosphere. The kind of place regulars go to sit and really listen.
- **Why go:** Legendary status among listening bar devotees. Close to Shibuya.

#### 8. Lion Cafe (Shibuya) — BONUS: Classical
- **Address:** 2-19-13 Dogenzaka, Shibuya-ku
- **Hours:** Daily 11am–10:30pm
- **Cover:** None (just buy a drink)
- **Vibe:** Not a "bar" per se, but Japan's oldest music kissa, open since 1926. Classical music only, played at high volume through vintage speakers. Mobile phones are banned. Talking is discouraged. Two-story space that feels like a baroque cinema. Go during the afternoon for a singular, meditative experience.
- **Why go:** Historical significance. A completely unique experience. Walking distance from Shibuya hotel.

### Jazz Bars

#### 1. Shinjuku Pit Inn — TOP PICK
- **Address:** Near Shinjuku-sanchome Station (2-min walk from exit C8, Toei Shinjuku Line)
- **Hours:** Daytime shows + evening shows
- **Cover:** Daytime: 1,300 yen (1 drink included); Weekends: from 2,500 yen; Evening: from 3,000 yen (1 drink included)
- **Vibe:** The legendary Tokyo jazz club. Operating since 1966 (reopened at current location 1992). The focus is Japanese jazz — avant-garde, free jazz, fusion, and straight-ahead. Basement setting, no-frills, all about the music. Late-night jam sessions are where the magic happens.
- **Why go:** The most important jazz club in Japan. Affordable cover. You will hear musicians you cannot hear anywhere else.

#### 2. Jazz Bar Samurai (Shinjuku) — INSTAGRAM REC
- **Address:** 3-35-5 Shinjuku, Shinjuku-ku, Tokyo, Mamoru Bldg 5F
- **Hours:** Evening (check current hours)
- **Cover:** Small table charge, drinks ~¥700
- **Vibe:** A husband-and-wife team have run this vinyl jazz bar since 1979. 2,500+ maneki-neko (lucky cats) line every surface — walls, cabinets, shelves. Owner Miyazaki-san collected them during his time in 1970s New York. Signed album sleeves on the walls. All vinyl jazz, picked by the owner. Super cozy, deeply quirky.
- **Why go:** There is literally no other bar like this on earth. The combination of 45+ years of history, vinyl jazz curation, and 2,500 lucky cats is otherworldly. Walking distance from Shinjuku Pit Inn — do both in one evening.

#### 3. Body & Soul (Aoyama)
- **Address:** Aoyama area, Minato-ku (look up current address — the club has moved before)
- **Hours:** Evening shows, typically 7:30pm and 9:30pm sets
- **Cover:** Around 4,000–7,000 yen depending on the act
- **Vibe:** Run by Kyoko Seki, one of the most important jazz promoters in Japan. Intimate room, exceptional booking. The artists who play here are top-tier. More refined than Pit Inn — think straight-ahead jazz and modern creative jazz.
- **Why go:** World-class jazz in an intimate setting. Check the schedule for your dates.

#### 3. Eagle (Yoyogi-Uehara)
- **Address:** Yoyogi-Uehara area
- **Hours:** Evening
- **Cover:** Drink minimum
- **Vibe:** One of the longest-running jazz kissaten in Tokyo (since 1967). Sleek, cool interior. Pure listening — jazz records on a premium system. No live music; this is a jazz kissa (listening cafe/bar). Darker, moodier atmosphere.
- **Why go:** The jazz kissa experience at its finest. Historical importance. A counterpart to the live jazz of Pit Inn.

#### 4. Pres Jazz Bar
- **Address:** Tokyo (confirm exact location)
- **Hours:** Evening
- **Cover:** Drink minimum
- **Vibe:** Dimly lit basement bar with leather stools and walls of vinyl records and CDs. Jazz-focused selection. Intimate, unpretentious, the kind of place where you sink into a chair and let the music wash over you.

#### 5. Blue Note Tokyo (Aoyama) — The Famous One
- **Address:** 6-3-16 Minamiaoyama, Minato-ku
- **Hours:** Two shows nightly (typically 7pm and 9:30pm)
- **Cover:** 5,000–15,000+ yen depending on artist
- **Vibe:** The Tokyo outpost of the famous NYC club. International headliners, polished sound, upscale dining. This is the opposite of a hidden gem — it is a well-oiled machine for premium jazz.
- **Why go:** Only if a specific artist you love is playing. Otherwise, Pit Inn and Body & Soul offer more authentic experiences at lower cost.

**Note on Gypsy Jazz in Tokyo:** There is no established, dedicated gypsy jazz/manouche jazz bar in Tokyo that surfaced in research. However, Pit Inn and Body & Soul occasionally book gypsy/manouche acts. Check their schedules closer to your dates. Also check tokyogigguide.com for any pop-up gypsy jazz events.

### Craft Cocktail Bars

#### 1. Bar Benfiddich (Nishishinjuku) — TOP PICK
- **Address:** 1-13-7 Nishishinjuku, Shinjuku-ku, Tokyo
- **Hours:** Two seatings: 7:00–8:55pm and 9:00pm onward
- **Price:** ~2,500–4,000 yen per cocktail
- **Reservations:** Opens on the 20th of each month via TableCheck. BOOK EARLY.
- **Vibe:** Ranked No. 18 in the World's 50 Best Bars 2025. Bartender Hiroyasu Kayama has no menu — he crafts drinks based on seasonal produce, many of which he grows himself on a farm outside Tokyo. Absinthe, herbal liqueurs, and botanical ingredients ground in a mortar and pestle at the bar. Dark, intimate, wizardly.
- **Why go:** One of the best bars on the planet. The "no menu" approach means every visit is unique. Absolutely unmissable for cocktail lovers.

#### 2. The SG Club (Shibuya) — TOP PICK
- **Address:** 1-7-8 Jinnan, Shibuya-ku
- **Hours:** Mon–Thu 11:30am–2:00am; Fri 11:30am–3:00am
- **Price:** ~2,000–3,500 yen per cocktail
- **Vibe:** Two-level concept by legendary bartender Shingo Gokan. Ground floor ("Guzzle") is casual highballs and quick drinks. Basement ("Sip") is the serious cocktail bar — darker, more intimate, with meticulous craft cocktails. Shibuya location makes it incredibly convenient from your hotel.
- **Why go:** Walking distance from Shibuya Granbell. World-class cocktails. Late hours. The two-floor concept means you can do casual or serious depending on your mood.

#### 3. Gen Yamamoto (Azabu-Juban)
- **Address:** 1-6-4 Azabu-Juban, Minato-ku
- **Hours:** Tue–Sun 3pm–11pm, Closed Monday
- **Price:** ~8,000–10,000 yen for an omakase course of 4–6 cocktails
- **Reservations:** Required
- **Vibe:** Cocktail omakase. Eight seats at a counter. Gen Yamamoto makes seasonal cocktails using Japanese fruits and ingredients with minimal sugar and no artificial flavors. Each drink is a revelation. The space is austere, minimal, almost spiritual.
- **Why go:** The most unique cocktail experience in Tokyo. Treat it like a fine dining reservation. Not a place to get drunk — a place to taste.

#### 4. The Bellwood (Shibuya)
- **Address:** 41-31 Udagawacho, Shibuya-ku
- **Hours:** 6pm–2am
- **Price:** ~2,500–4,000 yen per cocktail
- **Vibe:** Ranked No. 48 in World's 50 Best Bars 2025. Transforms traditional kaiseki cuisine concepts into cocktails. There is also a hidden sushi counter (Bell Sushi) in the back that serves inventive nigiri alongside exclusive mixed drinks. Stylish without being pretentious.
- **Why go:** Another Shibuya gem within walking distance. The kaiseki-cocktail concept is genuinely innovative. Book the sushi counter for a combined experience.

#### 5. Bar Trench / Bar Tram / Bar Triad (Ebisu)
- **Address:** Ebisu area (three sister bars within walking distance of each other)
- **Hours:** Evening–late
- **Price:** ~1,500–2,500 yen per cocktail
- **Vibe:** A trio of bars by Rogerio Igarashi Vaz. Bar Trench focuses on absinthe and herbal spirits. Bar Tram is the classic cocktail bar. Bar Triad is the experimental one. All three are in Ebisu, making for a perfect bar crawl. More affordable than the World's 50 Best places.
- **Why go:** A three-bar crawl in one neighborhood. Less hype, more substance. Ebisu is close to Shibuya.

### Live Music Venues (Psychedelic Rock / Jazz / Indie)

#### 1. UFO Club (Koenji) — TOP PICK for Psych Rock
- **Address:** Near Higashi-Koenji Station (basement of a building near a Family Mart at an intersection)
- **Phone:** 03-5306-0240
- **Cover:** Typically 1,500–2,500 yen
- **Vibe:** THE psychedelic rock venue in Tokyo. Interior designed by Shintaro Sakamoto (of Yura Yura Teikoku). The decor is like being inside a lava lamp. Books psych, noise, avant-garde, garage, and 60s/70s-inspired rock. This is the spiritual home of the Tokyo psych scene that produced Kikagaku Moyo.
- **Why go:** If you love Kikagaku Moyo, this is ground zero. Check tokyogigguide.com for the schedule in late April.

#### 2. Muryoku Muzenji (Koenji)
- **Address:** Under the railway tracks, Koenji
- **Cover:** ~1,500–2,000 yen
- **Vibe:** Tiny, cramped venue squeezed under the train tracks. Cat-themed decor everywhere. Virtually no PA system — the rawness is the point. Noise, experimental, punk, psych. If UFO Club is the lava lamp, this is the basement you weren't supposed to find.
- **Why go:** Maximally underground. Pair with UFO Club for a Koenji double-header.

#### 3. Koenji High (Koenji)
- **Address:** Near Koenji temple area
- **Cover:** ~1,500–2,500 yen
- **Vibe:** Less grungy than the others, more polished. Hosts indie, electropop, and alternative acts. Attached Amp Cafe is a chill space with local art and occasional mini shows. A good starting point if Muryoku Muzenji feels too raw.

#### 4. Basement Bar & THREE (Shimokitazawa)
- **Address:** Chazawa Dori, Shimokitazawa (below a liquor store and a doctor's clinic)
- **Cover:** ~1,500–2,500 yen
- **Vibe:** Two venues in one building. Basement Bar is indie, punk, and rock. THREE is more eclectic — experimental jazz, rockabilly, hip-hop alongside indie and punk. Capacity ~250 for THREE. Cheap drinks, packed schedule, enthusiastic staff.
- **Why go:** Something playing almost every night. Shimokitazawa is a natural pair with Little Soul Cafe for a vinyl + live music evening.

#### 5. Shelter (Shimokitazawa)
- **Address:** 2-6-10 Kitazawa, Setagaya-ku, Tokyo (Senda Bldg B1)
- **Cover:** ~2,000–2,500 yen
- **Vibe:** One of Shimokitazawa's most famous music clubs. At least one performance daily, usually hard rock. Dark, loud, sweaty basement energy.

**Kikagaku Moyo Note:** The band is on indefinite hiatus since December 2022. Frontman Tomo Katsurada launched solo project "Dream of the Egg" in 2024 (atmospheric folk/psych) but no 2026 tour dates are currently listed. Check tokyogigguide.com closer to your dates — Tokyo psych scene musicians frequently do unannounced or last-minute shows at UFO Club and Koenji venues.

### Late-Night Neighborhoods (Beyond Golden Gai)

#### 1. Koenji — TOP PICK (Most Authentic)
- **Getting there:** JR Chuo Line from Shinjuku (~10 min)
- **Vibe:** Tokyo's bohemian heart. Under the elevated train tracks, "Koenji Street" is lined with micro-bars. Full of underground live music venues. Tourists are genuinely rare here. Punk, psych, noise culture. Cheap drinks, eccentric locals, zero pretense.
- **Best for:** Live music (UFO Club, Muryoku Muzenji), authentic micro-bar hopping, feeling like you discovered something no guidebook told you about.

#### 2. Nonbei Yokocho / "Drunkard's Alley" (Shibuya)
- **Getting there:** 2-minute walk from Shibuya Station (right next to the Shibuya Granbell Hotel area)
- **Vibe:** Tiny alley of micro-bars, similar concept to Golden Gai but significantly less touristy. Locals still form the majority of patrons. Two strips — the main one and a more hidden strip with an even more local scene. The bars seat 5–8 people each.
- **Best for:** Convenience (steps from your hotel), intimate conversations with locals, bar-hopping without fighting crowds.

#### 3. Harmonica Yokocho (Kichijoji)
- **Getting there:** JR Chuo Line from Shinjuku (~15 min)
- **Vibe:** A maze of tiny drinking alleys rivaling Golden Gai and Nonbei Yokocho. Red lanterns beckon from micro-bars. Kichijoji is a beloved residential neighborhood — the crowd is local professionals and students. Much less touristy than Shinjuku.
- **Best for:** A full evening of bar-hopping in a neighborhood that feels genuinely local.

#### 4. Shimokitazawa
- **Getting there:** Keio Inokashira Line from Shibuya (~3 min)
- **Vibe:** Bohemian neighborhood known for vintage shops, live music, and indie culture. At night, the streets fill with young creatives bar-hopping between tiny establishments. The live music scene is the draw — combine Little Soul Cafe, Basement Bar, and whatever else you stumble into.
- **Best for:** Combining vinyl bars + live music + bar-hopping in one neighborhood.

#### 5. Ebisu Yokocho (Ebisu)
- **Getting there:** JR Yamanote Line from Shibuya (1 stop)
- **Vibe:** Alleyway maze of bars and restaurants. Less known by foreign tourists than Golden Gai. More food-focused than pure drinking — great for starting an evening with yakitori and beer before heading to the cocktail bars (Bar Trench/Tram/Triad are all in Ebisu).
- **Best for:** Food + drink combination evening. Cocktail bar crawl.

## KYOTO (Apr 30–May 4, staying at Shijo Kawaramachi)

### Vinyl / Listening Bars

#### 1. P.M. Sounds Kyoto — TOP PICK
- **Address:** 71-21 Nakagyo-ku, Kyoto (along Kiyamachi Street)
- **Hours:** Evening (confirm on arrival)
- **Cover:** Drink minimum
- **Vibe:** Kyoto's vinyl sanctuary. Shelves line the wall with jazz records (worn spines), city pop in bright pastels, rock, soul, electronic. Each record chosen with care. Intimate counter seating. Measured pours. The bartender curates the sound with the precision of a sommelier choosing wine.
- **Why go:** Walking distance from your hotel (Hotel Forza is right at Shijo Kawaramachi, P.M. Sounds is on nearby Kiyamachi). The most authentic listening bar experience in Kyoto.

#### 2. Ginza Music Bar Kyoto
- **Address:** Kyoto (opened summer 2025 — confirm exact location)
- **Hours:** Daily noon–midnight
- **Cover:** Check on arrival
- **Vibe:** The Kyoto branch of Tokyo's legendary Ginza Music Bar. Custom-built sound system, curated vinyl collection. Also features a sushi counter with an exclusive omakase course. More polished and upscale than P.M. Sounds.
- **Why go:** If you liked Ginza Music Bar in Tokyo, this is its Kyoto sibling. The sushi omakase + vinyl combo is unique.

#### 3. Good Morning Record Bar
- **Address:** Kyoto (near Kiyamachi area)
- **Hours:** Evening
- **Cover:** Minimal
- **Vibe:** Live DJs spinning an impressive selection of records while you sip affordable drinks in an intimate setting. More social and energetic than the quiet-listening format. One of Kyoto's most beloved bars.
- **Why go:** Affordable, fun, social. Good for a night when you want conversation alongside music.

#### 4. Record Sakaba Beatle Momo
- **Address:** Kyoto (confirm via Google Maps)
- **Hours:** Evening
- **Cover:** Check on arrival
- **Vibe:** Extensive vinyl selection. Fascinating to watch the staff read song requests and immediately locate the album among hundreds on the shelves. More casual and interactive than P.M. Sounds.

#### 5. Hachi Record Shop and Bar
- **Address:** Kyoto
- **Hours:** Check on arrival
- **Vibe:** Part record shop, part bar. Browse and buy vinyl while drinking. A good place to pick up records as souvenirs.

### Jazz Bars

#### 1. Hello Dolly (Pontocho) — TOP PICK
- **Address:** 161-2 Matsumoto-cho, Pontocho, Nakagyo-ku, Kyoto
- **Hours:** 6pm–12:30am
- **Cover:** Varies (check for live music nights)
- **Vibe:** Live jazz on Fridays and Saturdays — three sets at 8pm, 9:30pm, and 11pm, featuring Japanese talent. Views of the Kamo River. Located on atmospheric Pontocho alley. One of Kyoto's most storied jazz spots.
- **Why go:** Live jazz + river views + Pontocho atmosphere. Your Kyoto dates include Fri May 1 and Sat May 2 — perfect for catching live sets.

#### 2. Jazz Live Candy (Gion)
- **Address:** 224-1 Nakanomachi, Shinmonkomae, Hanamikoji, Higashiyama-ku, Kyoto (B1)
- **Hours:** 7:30pm–11:30pm
- **Cover:** Check on arrival (likely ~2,000–3,000 yen)
- **Vibe:** Located on Hanamikoji in Gion — the famous geisha district. Live music every night by talented musicians. Basement setting. Authentic and atmospheric, especially given the Gion location.
- **Why go:** Nightly live jazz. Combine with an evening walk through Gion.

#### 3. Zac Baran
- **Address:** South of Kyoto University (basement location)
- **Hours:** Evening
- **Cover:** Varies
- **Vibe:** Open since 1977 during Kyoto's jazz boom, one of the oldest jazz bars in the city. Hosts live performances and open mic/jam nights. Mixed crowd of students, professors, and jazz devotees. Warm, unpretentious basement atmosphere.
- **Why go:** Historical importance. Open mic nights mean you might catch something completely unexpected.

#### 4. Live Spot Rag
- **Address:** Kyoto (confirm exact location)
- **Hours:** Live sessions start earlier, transitions to mellow drinks later
- **Cover:** ~2,500–4,000 yen for live shows
- **Vibe:** Operating since the late 1980s. Books acoustic, fusion, jazz, and rock. International and Japanese acts. Intimate space that fills up quickly — buy tickets in advance.
- **Why go:** The most serious live jazz venue in Kyoto.

#### 5. Lush Life (near Demachiyanagi Station)
- **Address:** Near Demachiyanagi Station, Keihan Railway Line
- **Hours:** Open almost daily
- **Vibe:** Tiny jazz cafe run by Mr. and Mrs. Chaki. Very narrow space with counter seating only. Husband-and-wife operation. They help organize yearly piano recitals at nearby Kamigamo Shrine. Deeply personal, deeply local.
- **Why go:** About as intimate and authentic as jazz gets. The human connection.

- **Address:** Shijo-Karasuma / Shijo-Kawaramachi area, Kyoto
- **Hours:** By reservation only
- **Price:** Prix fixe — flight of 5 cocktails each paired with a snack

### Late-Night Neighborhoods

#### 1. Kiyamachi Street — TOP PICK
- **Vibe:** The main nightlife strip, running parallel to the Kamo River. Bars, clubs, restaurants. Comes alive at night with an electric energy. Your hotel (Hotel Forza Shijo Kawaramachi) is essentially ON this street — walk out the door and you are in the action.
- **Best for:** Spontaneous bar-hopping. Start at P.M. Sounds, walk to Bee's Knees, end at L'Escamoteur.

#### 2. Pontocho Alley
- **Vibe:** The narrow atmospheric alley running parallel to the river (one block west of Kiyamachi). Historic charm, lantern-lit. More restaurants than bars, but Hello Dolly and L'Escamoteur are here. Some bars have river-facing terraces.
- **Best for:** Pre-dinner atmosphere walk, jazz at Hello Dolly, cocktails at L'Escamoteur.

#### 3. Gion District
- **Vibe:** The geisha district. At night, it is atmospheric and moody — stone streets, wooden machiya buildings, occasional glimpse of a maiko. Jazz Live Candy is here. Less rowdy than Kiyamachi.
- **Best for:** Jazz + atmosphere. Combine with a walk through the historic streets.

**Kyoto closing times note:** Most Kyoto bars close between 11pm and 1am. A few on Kiyamachi stay open until 2–3am. This is NOT a late-night city like Tokyo or Osaka. Plan accordingly — if you want to stay out past midnight, stick to Kiyamachi.

## OSAKA (May 4–7, staying at Cross Hotel Osaka in Shinsaibashi)

### Vinyl / Listening Bars

#### 1. Bar Sanee — TOP PICK
- **Address:** Osaka (confirm exact location)
- **Hours:** Evening
- **Cover:** Drink minimum
- **Vibe:** Quietly stylish with a living room feel. Built around vintage 1950s–60s audio equipment. Vinyl library leans jazz with detours into fusion, pop, and beyond. The warmth of the old speakers is the star — you feel the music in your chest.
- **Why go:** The vintage audio equipment makes this feel like a time capsule. Intimate and special.

#### 2. Salvis Wine & Records (Tenma)
- **Address:** Tenma area, Osaka
- **Hours:** Evening
- **Cover:** Check on arrival
- **Vibe:** A wine and record shop with a SECRET BAR hidden behind a bookshelf door. Owner/sommelier Kazutomo Noguchi pays equal attention to wine quality and music crispness. Through the bookshelf is a luxurious lounge-style space.
- **Why go:** Hidden door behind a bookshelf. Wine + vinyl. The speakeasy element makes it an adventure.

#### 3. Music Bar Groovy
- **Address:** Osaka (confirm via Google Maps or Tripadvisor)
- **Hours:** Evening
- **Cover:** Check on arrival
- **Vibe:** Large collection focused on jazz and music from the 60s and 70s. The owner plays records on request. Social and friendly — Osaka listening bars tend to be more convivial than Tokyo's quiet-listening format. Conversation and laughter coexist with fidelity.
- **Why go:** The interactive, social Osaka energy applied to a listening bar. Request your favorites.

#### 4. Bar Jazz
- **Address:** Osaka (confirm location)
- **Hours:** Evening
- **Vibe:** A purist's hideout with crystal-clear sound from JBL 4550BK studio monitors and a bespoke CDS mixer. The sound quality is exceptional. Jazz-focused.
- **Why go:** Audiophile-grade jazz listening in Osaka.

#### 5. The Listening Room by OJAS at Patina Osaka
- **Address:** Patina Osaka hotel
- **Hours:** Check with hotel
- **Vibe:** Custom OJAS speakers (by Devon Turnbull) and a curated vinyl wall. High-fidelity sanctuary designed for focused listening sessions. More of a designed "experience" than a dive bar.
- **Why go:** If you appreciate high-end audio design. The OJAS speakers are works of art themselves.

#### 6. Milk Bar
- **Address:** Osaka
- **Hours:** Daily 7pm–1am
- **Cover:** Check on arrival
- **Vibe:** Good kickstart to a night out. Record bar with a more casual, social vibe.

#### 1. Canes & Tales at Waldorf Astoria Osaka

### Late-Night Neighborhoods

#### 1. Amerikamura (American Village) — TOP PICK
- **Getting there:** 5-minute walk south from Cross Hotel Osaka
- **Vibe:** Trendy, youthful, creative. Street art, quirky shops by day; lively bars and clubs by night. Triangle Park is the social hub. Bar Nayuta is here. Live performances, themed parties. The energy is younger and more creative than Dotonbori.
- **Best for:** Bar Nayuta + exploring the surrounding bars. Late-night energy without the Dotonbori tourist crush.

#### 2. Ura-Namba (Behind Namba)
- **Getting there:** 10-minute walk south from Cross Hotel
- **Vibe:** The "back streets" of Namba. Standing bars, tiny izakaya, local drinking culture. Where Osaka salarymen go after work. Gritty, authentic, cheap. The opposite of polished.
- **Best for:** Cheap standing-bar hopping. Authentic local Osaka drinking culture.

#### 3. Hozenji Yokocho
- **Getting there:** 10-minute walk from Cross Hotel (near Dotonbori but feels completely different)
- **Vibe:** ~80-meter atmospheric alley centered around Hozenji Temple. Traditional Japanese lanterns. ~50 restaurants ranging from cheap eats to Michelin-starred. Stone-paved, quiet, historic. The antithesis of neon Dotonbori despite being steps away.
- **Best for:** A moody, atmospheric dinner followed by a stroll. Your birthday dinner (May 6) at Matsuzaka Gyu Yakiniku M is in Hozenji-Hanare — explore the alley before or after.

#### 4. Tenma
- **Getting there:** JR from Osaka Station (~5 min) or walk from Umeda
- **Vibe:** Standing bars (tachinomi), cheap beer, unpretentious. Tenjinbashisuji shopping street by day transforms into a drinking district at night. Salvis Wine & Records is here. Very local crowd.
- **Best for:** Combining Salvis Wine & Records with standing-bar hopping.

#### 5. Kitahorie
- **Getting there:** ~20-minute walk west from Cross Hotel, or short subway ride
- **Vibe:** Quieter, more residential neighborhood with bars and restaurants at walkable distances from each other. Less chaotic than Shinsaibashi. The kind of place where you find a bar and stay a while.
- **Best for:** A mellower evening when you want quality over quantity.

## PRACTICAL TIPS

1. **Reservations:** Book Bar Benfiddich (opens 20th of prior month), Gen Yamamoto, and Nokishita 711 well in advance. Everything else is walk-in.
2. **Cover charges:** Budget 1,000–1,500 yen per listening bar, 2,000–5,000 yen per jazz club (with drink). Cocktail bars charge per drink (~2,000–4,000 yen).
3. **Cash:** Many small bars are cash-only. Carry at least 10,000 yen for a night out.
4. **Last trains:** Tokyo: ~midnight. Missing the last train means taxi or staying out until 5am (first train). Osaka is similar. Kyoto is earlier.
5. **Golden Week (Apr 29–May 6):** Expect popular bars to be busier than usual. Go early or go late (after 10pm) to avoid peak crowds.
6. **Etiquette at listening bars:** Some are strict about quiet listening (especially Lion Cafe). Others are more social (especially in Osaka). Read the room. Never talk over the music at a serious listening bar.
7. **Finding hidden bars:** Many bars have no signage. Use Google Maps in satellite/street view before you go. Screenshot the building entrance. Look for small signs in stairwells.
8. **Tokyogigguide.com:** Check this 1-2 weeks before departure for live music schedules at UFO Club, Pit Inn, and Shimokitazawa venues.
