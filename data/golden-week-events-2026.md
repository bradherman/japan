## KYOTO (April 30 - May 4)

### Festivals & Events

#### Fujinomori Matsuri (May 1-5)
- **Location:** Fujinomori Shrine, Fushimi ward (southern Kyoto)
- **Highlight:** Kakeuma Shinji on May 5 — acrobatic trick horse riding with 1,200 years of history. Riders gallop full tilt performing stunts like hanging off the saddle by a single foot or doing shoulder stands.
- **Other features:** Food stalls, portable shrines, taiko drum performances, samurai parade
- **Note:** You'll be in Kyoto May 1-3 for this; the main horse-riding event on May 5 falls after your departure to Osaka on May 4.

#### Yabusame at Shimogamo Shrine (May 3)
- **What:** Horseback archery (yabusame) with origins in the Heian period
- **Location:** Shimogamo Shrine
- **Significance:** Part of the pre-Aoi Matsuri ceremonies; archers shoot targets while riding at speed
- **Tip:** This is a Constitution Memorial Day holiday — expect large crowds. Arrive early.

#### Kamo Kurabeuma — Horse Racing at Kamigamo Shrine (May 5)
- **What:** Traditional horse race established in 1093, considered the origin of Japanese horse racing
- **Details:** Two riders in ceremonial court music costumes compete for speed while praying for world peace and abundant harvests
- **Note:** You depart Kyoto May 4, so you'll miss this by one day.

#### Kamogawa Odori — Geisha Dance Performance (May 1-22)
- **Location:** Pontocho Kaburenjo Theatre, Pontocho
- **What:** The last of Kyoto's spring geisha dances featuring geiko and maiko from Pontocho
- **Performances:** Three daily at 12:30, 14:20, and 16:10
- **Tickets:** Regular seats ¥4,000 | Special seats ¥6,000 | Special seats with tea ceremony ¥7,000
- **Note:** Your hotel (Forza Kyoto Shijo Kawaramachi) is walking distance from Pontocho. HIGHLY RECOMMENDED — this is a rare chance to see geiko and maiko perform.

#### Kodaiji Temple Spring Illumination (through May 6)
- **Location:** Kodaiji Temple, Higashiyama
- **Hours:** 17:00-22:00 (last entry 21:30)
- **What:** "Saki — Flowers Without Mind" — nighttime illumination with projection mapping. Marks 400 years since Nene's passing.
- **Admission:** ¥600 adults, ¥250 children
- **Tip:** Great evening activity. Higashiyama area is very walkable from your hotel.

#### Kyoto Nippon Festival 2026 (through May 24)
- **Location:** Kitano Tenmangu Shrine
- **What:** 10th anniversary event combining contemporary art, illuminations, and immersive theater
- **Features:** Photography/film installations by Mika Ninagawa; immersive theater "Grand Tea Gathering of the Flowered Night" (~60 min, 3 times daily)
- **Hours:** 9:00-20:30 (last entry 20:00)
- **Note:** Runs your entire Kyoto stay; worth a visit especially in the evening

### Temple & Shrine Special Events

#### Goryo Festival (May, multi-day)
- **Location:** Goryo Shrine
- **What:** Traditional ceremony to pray for peace and prosperity; historically the oldest among all Goryo Festivals in Japan
- **Duration:** 18-day celebration in May

### Markets

#### Toji Temple Flea Market (Kobo-san) — May 21
- **What:** Monthly market with ~1,200 stalls: antiques, ceramics, textiles, plants, handmade items
- **Note:** Falls after your departure from Kyoto — you'll miss this one.

#### Kitano Tenmangu Market (Tenjin-san) — May 25
- **What:** Monthly market with 250+ handmade-only stalls
- **Note:** Also after your departure.

**Market Alternative:** The Kyoto Nippon Festival at Kitano Tenmangu may have pop-up vendors during your visit.

### Crowd Warnings for Kyoto

- **May 3 (Constitution Memorial Day):** The single worst day. Fushimi Inari, Kinkaku-ji, Arashiyama Bamboo Grove, and Kiyomizu-dera will be extremely packed.
- **Avoid daytime visits to:** Kinkaku-ji, Fushimi Inari Taisha, Arashiyama Bamboo Grove on May 2-3
- **Strategy:** Hit popular temples at opening (many open 6:00-8:00 AM) or visit in the evening
- **Less crowded alternatives:**
  - Northern Higashiyama: Manshu-in, Shisen-do, Enko-ji (usually deserted)
  - Arashiyama: Walk north past Bamboo Grove to Gio-ji, Adashino Nembutsu-ji
  - Daitoku-ji and Myoshin-ji subtemples (very quiet)
- **Best day for popular spots:** April 30 or May 1 (weekdays before the holiday rush)

## OSAKA (May 4 - May 7)

### Festivals & Events

#### Nakanoshima Festival (May 3-5)
- **Location:** Nakanoshima Park, Kita Ward
- **What:** One of Japan's largest citizen-led festivals; 100+ local organizations
- **Features:** Handmade amusement park (human-powered merry-go-rounds, large mazes), stage performances, vibrant parades, bazaars, outdoor movie screenings
- **Admission:** Free
- **Note:** You arrive May 4, catching the last 2 days of this festival. The handmade amusement park is a uniquely Osaka experience.

#### Dotonbori Spring Festival (May 3-4)
- **Location:** Dotonbori shopping street (near your hotel, Cross Hotel Osaka)
- **What:** Art flea market, performance stages, live painting, karaoke competitions, food booths from Dotonbori vendors
- **Note:** You arrive May 4, so you may catch the tail end of this. Cross Hotel Osaka is right in the Dotonbori/Shinsaibashi area.

#### Koinobori (Carp Streamer) Display (April 27 - May 5)
- **Location:** Akutagawa Sakuratsutsumi Park
- **What:** 1,000 carp streamers displayed for Children's Day, symbolizing wishes for children's healthy growth
- **Note:** Last day is May 5, so you could see this on May 4 or 5.

#### Tsurumi Ryokuchi Food Festival (Golden Week)
- **Location:** Tsurumi Ryokuchi Park
- **What:** 40+ of Kansai's most loved eateries gather for a food festival
- **Note:** Great opportunity to sample a wide variety of Osaka/Kansai food in one place

#### Osaka Rose Festival (Early-Mid May)
- **Location:** Nakanoshima Park Rose Garden area
- **What:** Rose exhibitions, competitions, workshops, and rose seedling sales
- **Note:** Nakanoshima Park's rose garden has over 4,000 roses; peak bloom is early-to-mid May

#### Nemophila Blooms
- **Location:** Osaka Bay area
- **What:** A million nemophila (baby blue eyes) flowers blooming
- **Note:** Seasonal highlight; good for photos

### Food & Markets

- **Kuromon Market:** Open daily, ~150 shops selling fish, meat, and produce. Atmospheric for strolling and street food. Known as "Osaka's Kitchen."
- **Dotonbori Yatai Mura Matsuri:** Year-round festival food stall area in Namba recreating traditional Japanese festival atmosphere

### Crowd Warnings for Osaka

- **May 4-5:** Peak Golden Week days. Dotonbori and Shinsaibashi will be extremely crowded.
- **May 6 (Wed):** Substitute holiday — still busy but less than May 4-5
- **May 7:** Normal weekday — crowds significantly reduced
- **Strategy:** Your birthday dinner on May 6 at Matsuzaka Gyu Yakiniku is well-timed (reservation already secured). Use May 4-5 for outdoor festivals where crowds are part of the fun. Save indoor attractions and shopping for May 6-7.

## HAKONE (May 7 - May 9)

### Festivals & Events

#### Odawara Hojo Godai Festival (May 3)
- **Location:** Odawara Castle area, Odawara (gateway to Hakone)
- **What:** Odawara City's largest tourist event; 1,600-person warrior procession honoring the five Hojo lords
- **Features:** Samurai warrior parade (12:20-14:10), gun demonstrations, ninja performances, Odawara paper lantern dance, brass band
- **Access:** 10 min walk from Odawara Station
- **Note:** This falls on May 3 — you won't be in Hakone yet. However, you pass through Odawara Station on May 7 en route to Hakone, so the area will be calm by then.

#### Kintoki Shrine Festival (May 5)
- **Location:** Kintoki Shrine, foot of Mt. Kintoki
- **What:** Annual grand festival on Children's Day; prayers for children's healthy growth
- **Highlight:** "Yutate Shishimai" ritual — a performer in a shishi (guardian beast) mask stirs boiling water in a large cauldron and sprinkles it on visitors to ward off evil spirits
- **Note:** Falls before your arrival. You'll miss this.

#### Azalea & Rhododendron Season (Peak in May)
- **Location:** Hakone Gora Park (very close to your ryokan, Hakone Gora Byakudan)
- **What:** 1,000 azalea plants of 8 varieties + rhododendrons in peak bloom around the fountain
- **Additional:** Annual Azalea and Rhododendron Fair — 3,000 azalea plants (70 varieties) and 300 rhododendrons (40 varieties)
- **Tip:** This is a MUST — your ryokan is in the Gora area, so this is walkable. The park is at its most spectacular in May.

#### Green Festival (May 2026)
- **Location:** Hakone area parks
- **What:** Outdoor concerts and nature workshops

### Activities & Experiences

- **Hakone Open-Air Museum:** World-class outdoor sculpture park + Picasso collection. Opens at 9:00 AM — arrive early on May 8 to beat any remaining Golden Week stragglers.
- **Lake Ashi Pirate Ship Cruise:** Sightseeing cruise with views of Mt. Fuji (weather permitting). Post-Golden Week timing (May 7-9) means significantly fewer crowds.
- **Owakudani:** Volcanic valley with hot springs, sulfur vents, and the famous black eggs. Can be reached by Hakone Ropeway.
- **Onsen:** Your ryokan (Hakone Gora Byakudan) will have private onsen facilities; this is peak relaxation after the Golden Week crowds.

### Crowd Warnings for Hakone

- **GOOD NEWS:** By May 7-9, Golden Week is officially over. The substitute holiday (May 6) is the last day. You'll experience dramatically fewer crowds than if you were there May 2-6.
- **May 7:** Some returning travelers may still be on the Odawara Shinkansen platform, but Hakone itself should be calming down.
- **May 8-9:** Near-normal crowd levels. Ideal timing for the Hakone loop (Open-Air Museum, ropeway, pirate ship).

## TRANSPORT WARNINGS

### Shinkansen During Golden Week

- **CRITICAL:** During Golden Week, Tokaido and Sanyo Shinkansen "Nozomi" services operate with FULLY RESERVED seating — no unreserved cars available.
- Your April 30 Nozomi 23 (Tokyo 10:12 → Kyoto 12:23) is confirmed, which is great.
- Your May 7 Hikari ~10:10am → Odawara ~12:55pm is planned but not yet booked. Book this ASAP once the credit card issue is resolved. Hikari trains also fill up during Golden Week, though May 7 (post-holiday) should be easier.

### General Transport Tips

- **Avoid traveling on May 3 and May 6:** These are the peak outbound and return travel days for domestic Japanese travelers.
- **Your May 4 Kyoto→Osaka move:** This is Greenery Day. Consider taking local/regional trains (JR Special Rapid, ~30 min) rather than Shinkansen — the Shinkansen stations will be packed.
- **Kyoto buses:** Extremely crowded during Golden Week. Use the subway or walk when possible. Bus 206 (Kyoto Station loop) will be standing-room-only.
