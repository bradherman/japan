## 1. Festivals & Matsuri

### Akabane Baka Matsuri (68th Annual)
- **Dates:** April 25 (eve festival) & April 26 (main festival), 2026
- **Location:** JR Akabane Station East Exit area — Akabane Elementary School Grounds, Akabane Park, and Akabane Station East Exit Plaza
- **Admission:** Free
- **Highlights:** The famous "Baka Odori Parade" (Fool's Dance Parade), one of Kita Ward's largest events attracting hundreds of thousands of visitors. Local residents in happi coats carry mikoshi while dancing to flutes and festival chants. Food stalls, product exhibitions, disaster prevention fairs, and special stage performances (paid seating available).
- **Official site:** https://bakamatsuri.com/
- **Source:** [Tokyo Cheapo](https://tokyocheapo.com/events/akabane-baka-festival/)

### Kurayami Matsuri (Darkness Festival)
- **Dates:** April 30 - May 6, 2026
- **Location:** Okunitama Shrine, Fuchu City, Tokyo (about 30 min west of central Tokyo by train)
- **Admission:** Free
- **Highlights:** One of the largest and most significant festivals in all of Tokyo/Japan. Key events:
  - **May 3 "Koma-Kurabe":** Six horses run back and forth along the old Koshu-kaido road
  - **May 4 "Dashi Gyoretsu":** Procession of up to 22 decorated floats
  - **May 5 "Mikoshi Togyo":** The highlight — six large drums signal the start of fireworks, then eight portable shrines are carried to Otabisho in dramatic torchlit procession
- **Source:** [Tokyo Cheapo](https://tokyocheapo.com/events/kurayami-festival/), [Japan Travel](https://en.japantravel.com/tokyo/kurayami-matsuri-in-fuuchu/31002)

### Meiji Shrine Spring Grand Festival
- **Dates:** Late April - Early May 2026 (main ceremonies May 2-3)
- **Location:** Meiji Jingu (Meiji Shrine), Shibuya/Harajuku
- **Admission:** Free
- **Highlights:** The "Urayasu no Mai" dance (based on Emperor Showa's poem about world peace), plus traditional Noh and Kyogen performances — rare chance to see these performing arts in an outdoor shrine setting.
- **Source:** [GO TOKYO](https://www.gotokyo.org/en/story/guide/the-best-festivals-in-tokyo-and-japan/index.html)

**Note:** 2026 is a "super golden week" year — many workers take April 26 (Sun) through May 6 (Wed) as 11 consecutive days off. Expect heavy crowds at popular destinations. Book restaurants well in advance.

### Showa Kinen Park — Free Admission Day
- **Date:** April 29, 2026 (Showa Day)
- **Location:** Tachikawa, Tokyo (~30 min by train from Shinjuku)
- **Normal admission:** ~450 yen; FREE on this day
- **Highlights:** Ongoing "Flower Festival" (late March-May) with nemophila and poppies in full bloom across vast fields. Will be crowded.
- **Source:** [Showa Kinen Park](https://www.showakinen-koen.jp/guide-english/)

### NIKUFES (Meat Festival) 2026
- **Dates:** April 29 - May 10, 2026
- **Location:** Odaiba Aomi Area P Sector (1-1-16 Aomi, Koto City, Tokyo)
- **Access:** 2 min walk from Tokyo International Cruise Terminal (Yurikamome Line), 5 min from Daiba Station, 7 min from Tokyo Teleport Station (Rinkai Line)
- **Admission:** Free entry; meal tickets 700 yen each
- **Highlights:** Major outdoor meat festival — domestic wagyu and international dishes, stage performances, laid-back atmosphere. The start date (April 29) is "Niku no Hi" (Meat Day) in Japan.
- **Source:** [Tokyo Cheapo](https://tokyocheapo.com/events/niku-fes-tachikawa/), [Japan Travel](https://en.japantravel.com/tokyo/niku-fes-tokyo/61363)

### Children's Day (May 5)
- Colorful koinobori (carp-shaped windsocks) displayed everywhere — homes, parks, riverbanks, schools
- Traditional parades and markets at temples like Senso-ji
- Festival blessings and celebrations throughout the city

## 3. Cherry Blossom Status

**Verdict: Missed for Tokyo, but catch them up north.**

- Tokyo predicted first bloom: ~March 21, 2026
- Tokyo full bloom: ~March 28, 2026
- By late April, Tokyo's main cherry blossom season will be completely over

**Late bloomers / alternatives:**
- Some late-variety cherry trees (yaezakura) may still have petals in very late April
- **Sapporo (Hokkaido):** First bloom ~April 26, full bloom ~April 30 — if a day trip/detour is possible
- **Morioka/Aomori (Northern Honshu):** Peak around April 20-22

**Source:** [Tokyo Cheapo Sakura Forecast](https://tokyocheapo.com/living/tokyo-cherry-blossom-forecast/), [Nippon.com](https://www.nippon.com/en/japan-data/h02648/), [japan-guide.com](https://www.japan-guide.com/sakura/)
