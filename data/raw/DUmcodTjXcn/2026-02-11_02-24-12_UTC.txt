Golden Gai is one of the most recommended nightlife spots in Tokyo — and it can be fun.
But it’s also crowded, loud, and doesn’t always feel very local.

This is why I recommend Nakano instead.

You still get the character people are looking for — but with local bars, record shops, retro arcades, and a neighborhood vibe that feels lived-in rather than performative.

This is how I plan Tokyo nights:
by choosing places where locals actually spend time, not just what shows up on every list.
