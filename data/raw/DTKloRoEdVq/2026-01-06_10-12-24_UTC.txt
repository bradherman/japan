Tokyo in 2026 is seeing several major developments open across the city, especially around entertainment, culture, and large-scale urban projects.

PokéPark Kanto is opening near Yomiuriland, creating Tokyo’s first permanent Pokémon theme park and turning the area into a full entertainment destination.  In central Tokyo, Yurakucho Park is being developed as a new public and cultural space connecting Ginza and Tokyo Station, adding more open space and venues to the area.  Tokyo Dream Park in Ariake will open as a large entertainment complex focused on live events, exhibitions, and performances, strengthening the Tokyo Bay area as a destination. 
The Edo-Tokyo Museum in Ryogoku is reopening after a long renovation, with updated exhibitions focused on Tokyo’s history and urban development.  Takanawa Gateway continues to grow with MoN Takanawa, a new museum and cultural facility that adds public and cultural functions to the station area.  Shibuya’s redevelopment projects are still progressing, improving pedestrian flow, public spaces, and adding new buildings around the station.  Nihonbashi’s ongoing redevelopment brings new architecture and mixed-use buildings while keeping the area’s historical character.
 These are some of the main places shaping how Tokyo looks and functions in 2026, and they’re worth visiting if you want to understand how the city is evolving.
