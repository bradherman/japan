①
📍 NY Bistro by NoCode
Shin-Marunouchi Building 7F, 1-5-1 Marunouchi, Chiyoda-ku, Tokyo, Japan 

This Michelin-level bistro just opened in Marunouchi, Tokyo! At NY Bistro by NoCode, you can experience the flavors of NYC, created by Chef Fumio Yonezawa, the first Japanese sous-chef at Jean-Georges, a 3-star Michelin restaurant in NYC for 12 consecutive years.

This Premium Kobe Beef Burger is on another level! A 180g Kobe beef patty, crispy on the outside and juicy medium-rare inside, bursts with rich umami. It’s perfectly balanced with sweet Awajishima Mitsutama onions, a touch of Fuchu salt from Japan’s lunar tide, and fragrant Edo-grown herbs. Their homemade teriyaki sauce, crafted with Kagawa’s Kamebishi soy sauce, ties everything together beautifully. Each bite was so good and I can’t wait to try other burgers soon!

“Cashless Only”

②
📍BRISKSTAND ASAKUSA
2 Chome-9-16 Kotobuki, Taito City, Tokyo 111-0042, Japan

Tucked away in a quiet alley in Asakusa, this burger shop is changing the game for meat lovers.

They start with thick, juicy Wagyu patties and melty cheese—
but here’s the twist: they grill the cut burger flat-side down in its own juices!
The result? A crispy, golden crust and an explosion of flavor in every bite.

③
📍 WAGYU BROTHERS
Tokyo, Taito City, Asakusa 1-18-3 Endo Bldg. 2F

I just found a burger in Asakusa that literally surprised me.
It’s made with A5 Zabuton Wagyu steak.

The chuck cut is known for its buttery texture and rich flavor, and they grind it fresh into homemade patties. Every bite is insanely tender, packed with pure umami.
And that cheese… it doesn’t just melt, it falls over the burger in a glorious stream.

Don’t miss the crispy chicken side—it’s so thick and juicy, the crunch alone will hook you.

🍔A5 ZABUTON WAGYU STEAK BURGER
