Tokyo’s answer to New York’s most iconic sandwich 🥪

日本で最高の和牛パストラミを見つけた！

@bistronocode is chef @yone_asakusa’s love letter to the food he fell in love with during his tenure at 3-star Michelin restaurants in New York City. Bringing a casual dining spin to refined staples, their lunch menu features insanely good, jazzed-up wagyu burgers.

A hidden gem on their menu is this homemade A4 wagyu beef neck pastrami, packed with 300 grams of flavour. The beef is sourced from Ningyocho Imahan—a long-established, famous butcher in Tokyo. It’s brined, slow-cooked, and smoked with sakura wood for a deep, rich umami.

Topped with house-made sauerkraut infused with caraway seeds, this sandwich might just change the way you see pastrami.
