Save this for your Japan trip 🇯🇵

These 5 phrases will cover 80% of your daily life here, even if you know zero Japanese.

1 Arigatou gozaimasu = polite thank you. Use it for literally everything: shops, trains, restaurants, hotels.
2 Sumimasen = excuse me + sorry + “hi can I ask something?” It’s the safest way to get attention.
3 Onegaishimasu = the万能 word. It’s “please” but also “yes, that’s what I want” when you’re ordering or handing something over.
4 Daijoubu desu = “I’m good / it’s fine.” Great for refusing politely. Also means “are you okay?” so listen for it.
5 Wakarimasen = “I don’t understand.” Say it with a smile and people will usually slow down or switch to simple English.

In Japanese there’s a “polite” version (recommended for travel) and a “casual” version (still fine, just more informal).

1 Thank you
Polite: arigatou gozaimasu
Casual: arigatou

2 Excuse me / sorry / to get attention
Polite: sumimasen
Casual: gomen / gomen ne

3 Please / yes please / can you do this
Polite: onegaishimasu
Casual: onegai

4 I’m good / it’s fine / no thanks
Polite: daijoubu desu
Casual: daijoubu

5 I don’t understand
Polite: wakarimasen
Casual: wakannai

Tip: if you’re unsure, just use the polite one — it always lands well.
