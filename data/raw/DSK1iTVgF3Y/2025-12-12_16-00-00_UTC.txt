All the details 👇
Planning a trip to Japan and dreaming of wandering through Kyoto’s iconic bamboo forests? 🌿 Here’s the difference between the famous one you’ve seen all over IG… and the peaceful one just a short walk away.

✨ The Reveal:
That quiet, empty bamboo path you saw in our reel? It wasn’t Arashiyama… it was Adashino Bamboo Forest, only a 20-minute walk from the crowds.

If you want those serene, no-one-else-in-your-shot bamboo photos, head to Adashino right at opening (or a little before!).

🎋 Arashiyama Bamboo Grove (Kyoto’s most famous bamboo forest)
– Hours: Open 24/7
– Cost: Free
– Best for: Iconic Kyoto experience, easy access, nearby shops + Tenryu-ji Temple
– What to know: Always busy, especially from 8–11 am; trains drop visitors right into the district
– Photo tip: Sunrise is your best chance for fewer crowds, but it’s rarely empty

🎍 Adashino Bamboo Forest (the peaceful alternative 20 min away)
– Hours: 9:00–16:00 (varies slightly by season)
– Cost: ~¥500 per person
– Best for: Quiet paths, no crowds, beautiful light through the bamboo
– What to know: Located within Adashino Nenbutsu-ji Temple grounds, closes early, fewer tour groups
– Photo tip: Arrive at or before opening for dreamy, empty-path photos — this is the secret to crowd-free bamboo shots

If Japan is on your bucket list, save this for your Kyoto itinerary 🗺️🇯🇵
